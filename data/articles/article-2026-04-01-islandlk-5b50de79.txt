Down-and-up tendency continues to stifle share market

CSE was trending down during midday trading yesterday, even as brent futures dropped below US$110 per barrel, but the market slightly recovered at the end of the day’s trading, market analysts said.

Amid those developments both indices moved downward. The All Share Price Index went down by 11.91 points, while the S and P SL20 declined by 3.57 points. Turnover stood at Rs 3.57 billion with six crossings.

Those crossings were reported in JKH where 45.3 million shares crossed to the tune of Rs 811 6 million; its shares traded at Rs 17.90, Tokyo Cement 2.2 million shares crossed for Rs 196 million and its shares sold at Rs 88.90, Hayleys 345,000 shares crossed to the tune of Rs 74 million; its shares sold at Rs 215, VallibelOne 600,000 shares crossed for Rs 55.2 million; its shares sold at Rs 92, People’s Leasing two million shares crossed for Rs 44.6 million; its shares traded at Rs 22.30 and Seylan Bank (Non-Voting) 300,000 shares crossed for Rs 21 million; its shares traded at Rs 70.

In the retail market top seven companies that mainly contributed to the turnover were; JKH Rs 680 million (38 million shares traded), Sampath Bank Rs 218 million (1.5 million shares traded), HNB Rs 95 million (232,000 Shares traded), Commercial Bank Rs 92 million (448,000 shares traded), Tokyo Cement Rs 76 million (856,000 shares traded), Lanka IOC Rs 74 million (525,000 shares traded) and Prime Lands Residencies Rs 72 million (1.5 million shares traded). During the day 155 million share volumes changed hands in 23353 transactions.

It is said that the manufacturing sector, especially JKH, led the market, while the banking and finance sector also performed well, especially Sampath Bank. The Construction sector and the real estate sectors also performed significantly.

Yesterday the rupee was quoted at Rs 316.00/50 to the US dollar in the spot market, depreciating from Rs 315.60/80 the previous day, dealers said, while bond yields were broadly steady.
