Weather havoc convulsing the country exerts destabilizing impact on bourse

CSE trading yesterday was driven by some positive sentiments at the outset but later such trading momentum was arrested by the current emergency situation in the country stemming from the weather havoc convulsing the country.

Amid those developments both indices moved downwards. The All Share Price Index went down by 128.29 points while the S and P SL20 declined by 6.84 points.

Turnover stood at Rs 2.84 billion with eight crossings. Top seven crossings were; Hemas Holdings where 4.6 million shares crossed to the tune of Rs 159 million; its shares traded at Rs 34.80, HNB 211,000 shares crossed for Rs 81.30 million and its shares traded at Rs 85,Commercial Bank 300,000 shares crossed for Rs 53.7 million; its shares sold at Rs 179, Hayleys 200,000 shares crossed for Rs 37 million; its shares sold at Rs 184, hSenid Solutions 2 million shares crossed to the tune of Rs 34 million; its shares traded at Rs 17, Dialog Axiata 1.2 million shares crossed to the tune of Rs 33.6 million; its shares sold at Rs 28 and Access Engineering 288,000 shares crossed for Rs 21.2 million; its shares sold at Rs 73.50.

In the retail market top seven companies that mainly contributed to the turnover were; Colombo Dockyard Rs 167 million (1.2 million shares traded), HNB Rs 106 million (274,000 shares traded), Sampath Bank Rs 103.8 million (446,000 shares traded), Tokyo Cement Rs 102 million (995,000 shares traded), Access Engineering Rs 91 million (1.3 million shares traded), ACL Cables Rs 83.5 million (342,000 shares traded) and Commercial Bank Rs 75.5 million (389,000 shares traded). During the day 83 million share volumes changed hands in 29437 transactions.

It is said that the banking sector performed well, especially HNB and Commercial Bank, while the manufacturing sector, especially Hayleys, also did well. The IT sector also made its presence felt, especially hSenid.

Yesterday the upee opened flat at Rs 308.50/65 to the US dollar in the spot market from Rs 308.55/65 Friday, dealers said, while bond yields opened slightly higher.
