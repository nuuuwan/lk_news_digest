ASPI dips below 23,000 index points as days-long bull run fizzles out

The All Share Price Index dipped below 23000 index points yesterday after eleven days of bullishness consequent to selling pressure exerted by investors for higher returns, market analysts said.Further, the weakening of the rupee against the dollar also added insult to injury, analysts added.

Amid those developments both indices moved downward. The All Share Price Index went down by 111.13 points while the S and P SL20 declined by 7.57 points.

Turnover stood at Rs 3.82 billion with ten crossings. Those crossings were reported in Hatton Plantations where 10.9 million shares crossed to the tune of Rs 297.7 million; its shares traded at Rs 27.70, Commercial Bank 300,000 shares crossed for Rs 63.2 million and its shares traded at Rs 210.50, Pan Asia Bank 825,000 shares crossed to the tune of Rs 51.97 million; its shares sold at Rs 63.

Hayleys 213,695 million shares crossed to the tune of Rs 41.8 million; its shares traded at Rs 196, Sunshine Holdings 1.2 million shares crossed for Rs 40.8 million; its shares traded at Rs 34, Hemas Holdings 700,000 shares crossed for Rs 24.9 million; its shares sold at Rs 35.60 and Commercial Bank (Non -Voting) 110,000 shares crossed for Rs 21.2 million; its shares traded at Rs 193.

In the retail market top seven companies that mainly contributed to the turnover were; Renuka Agri Rs 212 million (24 million shares traded), Dialog Axiata Rs 194.4 million (6.5 million shares traded) Access Engineering Rs 150 million (2.2 million shares traded), JKH Rs 126 million (5.5 million shares traded), HNB Rs 109 million (265,761 shares traded), Seylan Developments Rs 101 million (2.9 million shares traded) and Kingsbury Rs 100 million (4.8 million shares traded). During the day 146.7 million share volumes changed hands in 37203 transactions.

The manufacturing and plantations sectors performed well despite the lower turnover level.

Commercial Bank and HNB in the banking sector, JKH in the manufacturing sector and Hatton Plantations in the plantation sector counters performed well.

Yesterday rupee opened at Ra 307.90/308.00 to the US dollar in the spot market stronger from Rs 308.10/30 the previous day, dealers said, but sharply depreciated from Rs 305.50/306.00 a week earlier, while bond yields were broadly steady.

Sri Lanka rupee had depreciated from 292.58 in December 2024, amid record current account surpluses and a sharply reduced budget deficit.

Macro-economists in the past blamed oil, cars and gold imports as well as exiting rupee bond holders for currency depreciation after cutting rates and boosting private credit, usually with inflationary open market operations as well.
