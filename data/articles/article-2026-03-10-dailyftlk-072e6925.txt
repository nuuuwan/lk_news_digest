US-Israeli strikes targeted at least 6,668 civilian units: Red Crescent

At least 6,668 civilian units have been targeted in US-Israeli attacks since the beginning of the war on 28 February, according to Iranian Red Crescent Society (IRCS) statistics cited by the Fars news agency.

During these attacks, a number of rescue and relief vehicles were also damaged, and several IRC staff were injured while carrying out rescue missions, the report said.

The death toll in Iran from the US-Israeli attacks has risen to 1,332, Iran’s semi-official Tasnim news agency is reporting.

The deadliest single incident occurred in the city of Minab in southeastern Iran, where a strike on an elementary girls’ school took place. Iranian Health Ministry Head of Public Relations Hossein Kermanpour said the Israeli attack on the girls’ school in Minab killed “about 180 young children.”

The IRCS also said at least 9,669 civilian units have been destroyed in US-Israeli attacks on Iran.

The statistics provided by the relief NGO included 7,943 residential units and 1,617 commercial units.

The US military confirmed that its death toll from the conflict has risen to six, after two bodies were recovered from a regional facility struck by Iran in addition to injuring 18. Israel has said 11 of its people have been killed.
