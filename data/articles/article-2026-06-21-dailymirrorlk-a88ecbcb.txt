India A beat Sri Lanka A by 66 runs to win Tri-Nation A-Series

June 21 (Daily Mirror) - India A defeated Sri Lanka A by 66 runs to clinch the Tri-Nation A-Series trophy at the Rangiri Dambulla International Stadium on Sunday.

After Sri Lanka A won the toss and elected to field, India A piled up a formidable 377 for 9 in their allotted 50 overs. The innings was headlined by a sensational knock from 15-year-old opener Vaibhav Sooryavanshi, who smashed 94 off just 29 balls, including a stunning half-century in only 11 deliveries. He narrowly missed out on a century but left the hosts under immense pressure with one of the most explosive innings seen at this level.

Captain Tilak Varma anchored the innings with a composed 67 off 90 balls, while Anukul Roy provided a late surge with an unbeaten 39 off just 15 deliveries, helping India A post a daunting target of 378.

In reply, Sri Lanka A fought hard but were bowled out for 311 in 46.2 overs. Sadeera Samarawickrama scored with 52, while Wanuja Sahan produced a spirited 62 to keep the chase alive. However, the required run rate continued to climb as India A's bowlers struck at regular intervals.

Yash Thakur led the bowling attack with three wickets, while Anukul Roy and Vipraj Nigam claimed two wickets each to derail Sri Lanka A's chase and seal a comprehensive 66-run victory, handing India A the Tri-Nation A-Series title.
