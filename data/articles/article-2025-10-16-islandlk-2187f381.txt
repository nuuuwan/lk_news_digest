Lively share trading led by Hemas Holdings helps revive CSE; turnover crosses Rs 8 billion

The CSE yesterday bounced back after the previous day’s setback due to 17 crossings and steady retail trading, market analysts said.Amid those developments both indices moved upwards. The All Share Price Index went up by 136.16 points, while the S and P SL20 rose by 34.91 points. Turnover stood at Rs 8.87 billion with 17 crossings.

Top seven crossings were; Hemas Holdings 35 million shares crossed to the tune of Rs 1.19 billion and its shares traded at Rs 35, DFCC 5.6 million shares crossed for Rs 853 million and its shares traded at Rs 155, HNB (Non-Voting) 1.1 million shares crossed for Rs 347 million; its shares traded at Rs 310, Central Finance 993,000 shares crossed for Rs 293 million; its shares sold at Rs 295, DIMO 109,000 shares crossed for Rs 256 million; its shares traded at Rs 2350, Seylan Bank 1.99 million shares crossed to the tune of Rs 210 million; its shares sold at Rs 105 and TJ Lanka three million shares crossed for Rs 123 million; its shares traded at Rs 41.

In the retail market top seven companies that mainly contributed to the turnover were; Hemas Holdings Rs 470 million (13 million shares traded), LVL Energy Fund Rs 260 million (21 million shares traded), Central Finance Rs 202 million (683,000 shares traded), DFCC Bank Rs 186 million (1.2 million shares traded), Lanka IOC Rs 163 million (1.2 million shares traded), RIL Properties Rs 136 million (four million shares traded) and Dipped Products Rs 134 million (1.97 million shares traded). During the day 294 million share volumes changed hands in 36000 transactions.

It is said that the manufacturing sector, especially Hemas, led the market, while the banking and financial sector performed well, especially DFCC Bank. Further, the real estate sector also performed well.

Yesterday the rupee opened flat at Rs 302.95/303.00 to the US dollar, while bond yields were broadly steady, dealers said.

The telegraphic transfer rates for the American dollar were 299.5000 buying, 306.5000 selling; the British pound was 401.4427 buying, and 412.8045 selling, and the euro was 347.3565 buying, 358.7197 selling.
