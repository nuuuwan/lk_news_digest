Two weeks’ ceasefire announcement in M-E buoys bourse

The CSE closed yesterday on a bullish sentiment due to the two weeks’ ceasefire announcement in the Middle East, thus enabling the banking sector to push turnover to a satisfactory level.

Amid those developments both indices moved upwards. The All Share Price Index was up 4.21 percent, or 885.14 points at 21,917.60, while the S&P SL20 was up 4.42 percent, or 257.68 points at 6,090.50.

Turnover stood at Rs 6.61 billion with 11 crossings. Top seven crossings were: NDB 3.6 million shares crossed to the tune of Rs 390 million; its shares traded at Rs 108, JKH 4.8 million shares crossed to the tune of Rs 94.8 million; its shares traded at Rs 19.70, Commercial Bank 405000 shares crossed for Rs 85.5 million; its shares traded at Rs 210.

Lanka IOC 591,000 shares crossed for Rs 83.4 million; its shares sold at Rs 141, Pan Asia Bank 1.5 million shares crossed to the tune of Rs 76.5 million; its shares traded at Rs 51, Singer (Sri Lanka) 500,000 shares crossed for Rs 41.5 million; its shares sold at Rs 83 and Prime Lands Residencies 800,000 shares crossed to the tune of Rs 40.8 million; its shares sold at Rs 51.

In the retail market companies that mainly contributed to the turnover were; JKH Rs 363 million (18.4 million shares traded), Singer (SriLanka) Rs 342 million (four million shares traded), Softlogic Capital Rs 337 million (21.2 million shares traded), LMF Rs 288 million (3.2 million shares traded), Colombo Dockyard Rs 276 million (two million shares traded), NDB Rs 249 million (2.2 million shares traded) and Prime Lands Residencies Rs 233 million (4.5 million shares traded). During the day 247.8 million shares volumes changed hands in 41038 transactions.

JKH (up 6.49 percent at Rs.19.70), Commercial Bank (up 5.01 percent at Rs.209.75), Hatton National Bank (up 3.95 percent at Rs.414.00), Dialog Axiata (up 6.58 percent at Rs.32.40), and Colombo Dockyard (up 11.27 percent at Rs.138.25) were top positive contributors to the ASPI.

It is said that capital goods saw a significant performance, especially JKH. The banking sector drove the market, especially NDB.

Yesterday the rupee was quoted at Rs 315.10/25 to the US dollar in the spot market, recovering from Rs 315.40/50 the previous day, dealers said, while bond yields dropped drastically following the ceasefire announcement.
