SriLankan Airlines reports fraudulent payments in UAE and India

SriLankan Airlines says it has been hit by two separate fraud-related incidents involving overseas payments, including a suspected business email compromise in the UAE and misappropriation of funds at its Chennai office in India.

In a statement issued yesterday, the airline said a payment of AED 974,000 to a Dubai-based service provider had been diverted after the service provider’s email account was allegedly compromised by a third party, who changed bank account details and provided supporting documents through official communication channels.

The airline said it had immediately halted further payments to the account and reported the matter to authorities in the UAE and Sri Lanka’s Criminal Investigation Department. Investigations are continuing in both countries.

SriLankan Airlines maintained that the payment had been made based on authorised instructions and documentation later found to have originated fraudulently from the compromised email account. The airline also denied liability for the service provider’s non-receipt of funds.

Separately, the airline said several Indian nationals attached to its finance division in Chennai were suspected of misappropriating about INR 22 million over a period of time by allegedly altering invoices, payment details and signatures.

SriLankan Airlines said its Colombo head office detected unusual payments and launched an internal investigation, after which the employees concerned stopped reporting to work. The matter has now been referred to Indian law enforcement authorities, while efforts are underway to recover the losses.
