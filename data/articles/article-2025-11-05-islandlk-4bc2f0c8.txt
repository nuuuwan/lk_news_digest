Ebullience in CSE trading enables bourse to hit 23,000 index points

CSE activities were extremely bullish yesterday following the touching of 23000 index points by the CSE following the receipt of satisfactory quarterly results plus positive macroeconomic fundamentals.

But later the market witnessed some profit- takings and began to fall significantly.

Both indices moved upwards; the All Share Price Index went up by 46.54 points, while the S and P SL20 rose by 3.71 points.

Turnover stood at Rs 4.4 billion with six crossings. Those crossings were reported in Aitken Spence where 2.38 million shares crossed to the tune of Rs 387.8 million; its shares traded at Rs 163, Dipped Products 1.3 million shares crossed for Rs 87.1 million and its shares traded at Rs 67, Commercial Bank 300,000 shares crossed for Rs 62.4 million; its shares traded at Rs 208, Sunshine Holdings 1.25 million shares crossed for Rs 45.6 million; its shares traded at Rs 37, Swisstec 220,000 shares crossed to the tune of Rs 20 million; its shares sold at Rs 92.50 and Access Engineering 325,000 shares crossed to the tune of Rs 20 million; its shares fetched Rs 51.60.

In the retail market companies that mainly contributed to the turnover were; Sunshine Holdings Rs 346 million (9.6 million shares traded), Colombo Dockyards Rs 292.6 million (750,000 shares traded) Renuka Agri Farms Rs 169 million (20.7 million shares traded), Digital Mobility Solutions Rs 159 million (one million shares traded), Lanka Milk Food Rs 151 million (2.1 million shares traded), JKH Rs 137 million (6.4 million shares traded) and JKH Rs 106 million. During the day 161.7 million shares volumes changed hands in 36993 transactions.

It is said that manufacturing and agriculture sector counters gave some impetus to the CSE while the banking sector was also a bit active, especially Commercial Bank. Sunshine Holdings, Digital Mobility Solutions, Aitken Spence and few other companies showed mixed reactions.

Yesterday the rupee opened weaker at Rs 304.54/60 to the US dollar in the spot market from 304.45/55 the previous day, dealers said, while bond yields were broadly steady.
