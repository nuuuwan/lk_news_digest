Share market dips slightly due to profit-takings but records over Rs 7 billion turnover

The CSE recorded a 1800 points gain during the last 14 working days but yesterday the market witnessed a drop due to slight profit- taking, market analysts said.

Amid those developments both indices moved downwards. The All Share Price Index went down by 65.24 points, while the S and P SL20 declined by 26.90 points.

Turnover stood at Rs 7.32 billion with 14 crossings. The top seven crossings were; Cargills 881,000 shares crossed to the tune of Rs 661 million; its shares traded at Rs 750, CT Holdings 728,000 shares crossed to the tune of Rs 474 million; its shares traded at Rs 651, Commercial Bank two million shares crossed to the tune of Rs 389 million; its shares sold at Rs 196.50, Colombo Dockyards 1.6 million shares crossed for Rs 219 million; its shares traded at Rs 136, Chevron Lubricants one million shares crossed to the tune of Rs 180 ; its shares sold at Rs 180, DFCC 400,000 shares crossed for Rs 65.2 million; its shares traded at Rs 163 and JKH three million shares crossed for Rs 60.2 million’; its shares sold at Rs 21.40.

In the retail market top seven companies that mainly contributed to the turnover were; Commercial Bank Rs 293 million (1.5 million shares traded), LCB Finance Rs 267 million (32 million shares traded), JKH Rs 267 million (12.4 million shares traded), HNB (Non-Voting) Rs 216 million (700,000 shares traded), Colombo Dockyards Rs 172 million (1.3 million shares traded) Sunshine Holdings Rs 153 million (4.7 million shares traded) and Pan Asia Power Rs 127 million (3.9 million shares traded). During the day 189 million shares volumes changed hands in 38497 transactions.

It is said that the banking and financial sector performed well at the floor, especially Commercial Bank, while the manufacturing sector, especially Chevron and JKH, was also significantly active in the market.

Yesterday, the rupee opened at Rs 302.55/60 to the US dollar, slightly weaker from Rs 302.52/59 the previous day, while bond yields were broadly steady, dealers said.

A bond maturing on 15.09.2027 was quoted at 8.85/95 percent, up from 8.85/90 percent.

A bond maturing on 15.12.2032 was quoted at 10.50/60 percent, from 10.50/55 percent.

An auction of 33,500 million rupees Treasury bills was ongoing.

The telegraphic transfer rates for the American dollar was 299.0000 buying, 306.0000 selling; the British pound was 399.4985 buying, and 410.8603 selling, and the euro was 345.2228 buying, 356.5860 selling.
