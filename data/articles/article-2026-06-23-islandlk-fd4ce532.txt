Despite mid-session hiccups CSE’s trading tempo picks-up

Both CSE indices moved up yesterday despite stock trading encountering some hiccups in mid-session. The All Share Price Index went up by 2.97 points, while the S and P SL20 rose by 5.23 points. Turnover stood at Rs 1.56 billion with four crossings.

Those crossings were: Hemas Holdings crossed 6.2 million shares to the tune of Rs 203 million and its shares traded at Rs 32.50, NDB 1.5 million shares crossed for Rs 155 million; its shares traded at Rs 110, HNB (Non-Voting) 100,000 shares crossed for Rs 33.7 million; its shares sold at Rs 337.50 and CDB 500,000 shares crossed for Rs 24 million; its shares sold at Rs 44.

In the retail market top seven companies that mainly contributed to the turnover were: Hayleys Fiber Rs 97 million (610,000 shares traded), Pan Asia Power Rs 88 million (5.5 million shares traded), JKH Rs 59 million (2.9 million shares traded), Softlogic Life Rs 57 million (601,000 shares traded), Pan Asia Bank Rs 35 million (644,000 shares traded), LB Finance Rs 28 million (152,000 ,shares traded) and Access Engineering Rs 24 million (304,000 shares traded). During the day 65 million share volumes changed hands in 16230 transactions.

It is said that the capital goods sector boosted the market, especially Hemas Holdings and Hayleys Fiber. Further banking sector counters, especially NDB and Pan Asia Bank and several other banks performed well.

Yesterday the rupee was quoted at Rs 334.40/335.00 to the US dollar in the spot market, weaker from Rs 334.50/75 the previous day, while bond yields were edging lower on the shorter tenors, dealers said.

The telegraphic transfer rate for Sri Lanka’s rupee against the US dollar was Rs 330.00 buying, Rs 339.00 selling; the euro was Rs 374.6873 selling, Rs 388.6043 buying; and the pound was Rs Rs 436.1036 buying, 450.1492 selling.
