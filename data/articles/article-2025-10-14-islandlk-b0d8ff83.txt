Hong Kong-based World Elite Ltd acquires 50.01 per cent ordinary shares in Gestetner of Ceylon; market overall positive

A Hong Kong based entity, World Elite Limited, has acquired 50.01 percent of the issued ordinary shares of Gestetner of Ceylon Plc.

According to the CSE, 1,329,115 ordinary shares were bought at Rs 239.75 per share for Rs 318 million (Rs 318,655,321.25).

The balance 65,920 shares were purchased from the CSE on October 13.

Under this transaction the company owns more than 50 percent of shares in Gestetner.

Amid those developments, the market performance was overall positive, owing to halt in increasing electricity tariffs for next two months by the Public Utilities Commission.

This had given an impetus to the market. The All Share Price Index went up by 70.85, while the S and P SL20 rose by 22.07 points.

Turnover stood at Rs 5.74 billion with 13 crossings. Top seven crossings were reported in Hemas Holdings; 4.8 million shares crossed to the tune of Rs 159 million; its shares traded at Rs 32.50, Ceylon Land Equity 8.7 million shares crossed to the tune of 114 million; its shares traded at Rs 13, Cargills Ceylon 110,000 shares crossed for Rs 86 million; its shares sold at Rs 786, CT Holdings 88000 shares crossed to the tune of Rs 58 million; its shares sold at Rs 657, Royal Ceramics one million shares crossed for Rs 46.5 million; its shares sold at Rs 46.50, JKH 1.1 million shares crossed for Rs 46 million; its shares sold at Rs 21.60 and Haylays 150,000 shares crossed for Rs 28.50 million; its shares sold at Rs 190.

In the retail market top seven companies that mainly contributed to the turnover were; WindForce Rs 309 million (7.3 millionshares traded), Ambeon Capital Rs 234 million (5.8 million shares traded), Pan Asia Power Rs 213 million (6.9 million shares traded), People’s Leasing Rs 183 million (6.3 million shares traded, Associated Motor Finance Rs 145 million (107 million shares traded) , Raigama Wayamba Salterns Rs 111 million (3.86 million shares traded) and Brown’s Investments Rs 104 million (13.7 million shares traded). During the day, 391 million shares volumes changed hands in 44000 transactions.

The energy sector was a bit active at the floor, especially WindForce, while the manufacturing sector, especially Hemas Holdings and JKH, were active in the market as well.

Yesterday, the rupee opened at Rs 302.60/70 to the US dollar, weaker from 302.59/62 the previous day, while bond yields were broadly steady, dealers said.
