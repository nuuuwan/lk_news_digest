Tired eyes…

The heat we are now experiencing, especially during the day, is awful, and many are complaining of the glare affecting their eyes.

Well, let’s hope the following remedies will do the needful:

Place chilled cucumber slices over your eyelids for 10-15 minutes. This can help reduce puffiness and refresh the delicate skin around your eyes.

Use a soft cloth, soaked in cold water, or chilled green tea bags. Green tea can reduce swelling and can also help tighten and lift the skin around your eyes. Place the compress over your eyelids for 10 minutes while you relax.

Aloe Vera is nature’s answer to tired, puffy eyes. Gently dab fresh Aloe Vera juice around your eyes to hydrate the skin and reduce puffiness.

Take a spoon and leave it in the freezer for about 10-15 minutes and then place the hollow side of that chilled spoon onto your eyes to relieve puffiness. This chilled spoon will immediately soothe tired eyes—the cold temperature will tighten the skin around your eyes and also contract the blood vessels to reduce puffiness.

Excessive screen time strains your eyes and contributes to fatigue. To counter this, every 20 minutes, take a 20-second break and look at something 20 feet away. This simple practice can help reduce eye strain by allowing your eyes to relax and refocus, keeping them feeling fresh.

Dehydration can make your eyes look tired and sunken. Make sure you’re drinking plenty of water throughout the day. Add cucumber, lemon, or mint slices to your water for a refreshing twist, motivating you to drink more.

And when you go out in this blazing sun, whenever possible, wear sunglasses!
