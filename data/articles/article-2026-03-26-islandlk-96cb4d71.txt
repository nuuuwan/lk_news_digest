Food, beverage and tobacco sectors lead CSE’s positive spurt

The CSE yesterday opened on a positive note with food, beverage and tobacco sectors leading the turnover due to the virtual cooling off period stemming from President Donald Trump’s proposed ceasefire in the Middle East, market analysts said.

Brent futures dropped below US $95 per barrel, as reports of diplomatic efforts to end the war began to circulate, including a proposal by Trump for a one-month ceasefire.

Amid those developments both indices moved upwards. The All Share Price Index went up by 797 points, while the S and P SL20 rose by 228 points.

Turnover stood at Rs 6.4 billion with 10 crossings. The top seven crossings were: Melstacorp 2.5 million shares crossed to the tune of Rs 428 million; its shares traded at Rs 174, LMF 2.7 million shares crossed for Rs 218 million; its shares traded at Rs 81, Singer (SriLanka) 1.2 million shares crossed for Rs 93 million; its shares sold at Rs 78, ACL Cables 800,000 shares crossed for Rs 72 million; its shares sold at Rs 90, Colombo Dockyard 500,000 shares crossed to the tune of Rs 59 million; its shares fetched Rs 119.25, LB Finance 300,000 shares crossed to the tune of Rs 43 million; its shares sold at Rs 143.50 and RIL Properties one million shares crossed to the tune of 27 million; its shares fetched Rs 27.50.

In the retail market top seven companies that mainly contributed to the turnover were; Acme Printers Rs 464 million (76 million shares traded), Softlogic Capital Rs 367 million (24 million shares traded), Colombo Dockyard Rs 298 million (2.2 million shares traded), JKH Rs 249 million (12 million shares traded), LMF Rs 247 million (2.9 million shares traded), Prime Lands Residencies Rs 238 million (4 million shares traded) and Singer (SriLanka) Rs 194 million (2 million shares traded). During the day 299 million share volumes changed hands in 47000 transactions.
