Bourse enlivened by positive ripple effects from reducing M-E tensions

CSE trading experienced some positive ripple effects from reducing Middle East tensions yesterday, especially in relation to the announcement of the Israel-Lebanon ten day ceasefire agreement.

Amid those developments both local and foreign investors were became rather optimistic. The All Share Price Index went up by 212.25 points, while the S and P SL20 rose by 54.83 points. Turnover stood at Rs 4.97 billion with nine crossings.

The first seven crossings were: NDB 1.8 million shares crossed to the tune of Rs 211 million and its shares traded at Rs 115, LOLC Holdings 149,000 shares crossed for Rs 85.4 million; its shares traded at Rs 573, Hemas Holdings 1.7 million shares crossed for Rs 53 million; its shares traded at Rs 32.30, JKH 2.3 million shares crossed to the tune of Rs 46.9 million; its shares sold at Rs 20.40, People’s Leasing and Finance 1.7 million shares crossed for Rs 42.5 million; its shares traded at Rs 25, CIC Holdings one million shares crossed to the tune of Rs 34 million; its shares traded at Rs 34 and NTB 100,000 shares crossed for Rs 31.5 million; its shares traded at Rs 315.

In the retail market top seven companies that mainly contributed to the turnover were; hZenid Rs 218 million (8.7 million shares traded), Softlogic Holdings Rs 207 million (2.3 million shares traded), Softlogic Finance Rs 173 million (22.1 million shares traded), Lanka IOC Rs 170 million (1.18 million shares traded), Hemas Holdings Rs 151 million (4.7 million shares traded), Asiri Surgical Hospital Rs 127 million (5.6 million shares traded) and Ceylon Land Equity Rs 122 million (10.5 million shares traded). During the day 221.6 million share volumes changed hands in 42140 transactions.

It is said that banking and financial sector stocks performed well, especially NDB and Softlogic Finance. Further IT solutions sector, especially hZenid, performed well.

Meanwhile, Commercial Bank said it has listed 14,236,841 voting shares and 984,260 non-voting shares after a scrip dividend. Voting stock was down Rs 1.25 at Rs 210.00 during intra-day trading.

Browns Beach Hotels said trading of the securities will resume from April 17 and continue until April 27 for 7 market days.

“Upon the conclusion of the period, trading of the securities of the Company shall be suspended in terms of Section 14.1.3 of the CSE Listing Rules.” The stock was up Rs 4.80 at Rs 28.

Yesterday the rupee was quoted at Rs 316.05/15 to the US dollar in the spot market, weaker from Rs 315.85/316.10 the previous day, dealers said, while bond yields were quoted steady.
