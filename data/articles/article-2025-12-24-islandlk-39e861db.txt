Market rebounds on Indian aid news, ASPI gains 28.80 points

The Colombo Stock Exchange (CSE) experienced a session of two variants yesterday, ultimately closing higher after a mid-session dip was reversed by significant positive news from India.

A decisive upturn followed the announcement from India’s External Affairs Minister, Dr. S. Jaishankar, confirming a US $450 million financial package for reconstruction in flood-affected areas of Sri Lanka.

Bolstered by this development, both key indices climbed. The All Share Price Index (ASPI) gained 28.80 points, while the S&P SL20 rose by 5.79 points. The day’s turnover reached Rs. 2.56 billion, supported by six major crossings.

Those crossings were reported in EML Consultants, which crossed 27 million shares to the tune of Rs 116 million and its share price traded at Rs 4.30, Hemas Holdings 2.6 million shares crossed to the tune of Rs 88.4 million and its share price traded at Rs 34, Sampath Bank 500,000 shares crossed to the tune of Rs 71 million and its share price traded at Rs 142, Richard Pieris 1.2 million shares crossed to the tune of Rs 50.7 million and its share price traded at Rs 41, Royal Ceramics one million shares crossed to the tune of Rs 44 million and its share price traded at Rs 44 and JKH two million shares crossed to the tune of Rs 42 million and its share price traded at Rs 21.

In the retail market top seven companies that have mainly contributed to the turnover were Colombo Dockyard Rs 368 million (3.4 million shares traded), Colombo Dockyards (Right Issue) Rs 287 million (5.8 million shares traded), JKH Rs 151 million (7.2 million shares traded), Capital Alliance Rs 101 million (1.6 million shares traded), Haylays Rs 72 million (375,000 shares traded), Aman Tackful Rs 64 million (2.9 million shares traded) and Commercial Bank Rs 54 million (280,000 shares traded). During the day 102.2 million shares volumes changed hands in 23952 transactions.

The market showed a mixed reactions especially manufacturing and financial sector performed well. Further banking sector counters especially Sampath Bank and Commercial Bank active at the floor.
