Ceylon Chamber announces leadership transition; Shiran Fernando appointed SG/CEO

The Ceylon Chamber of Commerce has announced a planned leadership transition, appointing Shiran Fernando as its new Secretary General and Chief Executive Officer, effective May 8, 2026. He will succeed Buwanekabahu Perera, who concludes a three-year tenure marked by institutional strengthening and expanded international engagement.

Chairperson Krishan Balendra said the transition reflects the Chamber’s emphasis on governance, continuity, and structured succession planning. He noted that the organisation is well-positioned to maintain stability while adapting to the evolving needs of its membership and the broader economy.

Perera’s tenure is highlighted by efforts to reinforce the Chamber’s governance framework and elevate its policy credibility, alongside strengthening ties with government, international stakeholders, and the private sector. His leadership extended beyond the Chamber through his role as Chair of the Indian Ocean Rim Association Business Forum, where he helped enhance Sri Lanka’s private-sector engagement across the Indian Ocean region, boosting regional business linkages and economic diplomacy.

Fernando’s appointment signals continuity, given his nearly decade-long involvement with the Chamber’s policy and research functions. Having served as Chief Economist, Head of the Economic Intelligence Unit, and most recently Chief Economic Policy Advisor, he has played a central role in shaping policy positions and engaging with national economic reform processes. His familiarity with the Chamber’s governance structures and stakeholder network is expected to ensure a smooth transition and sustained strategic direction.

Institutional stability is further supported by Alikie Perera, who continues as Deputy Secretary General, Chief Operating Officer, Financial Controller, and CEO of GS1 Lanka. With over 30 years of service across multiple leadership cycles, she has been instrumental in maintaining operational and financial discipline, while helping develop key national platforms such as the Sri Lanka Economic and Investment Summit and the Best Corporate Citizens Awards.

Overall, the transition underscores the Chamber’s focus on continuity, institutional resilience, and long-term value creation, positioning it to play a sustained role in supporting Sri Lanka’s economic development.
