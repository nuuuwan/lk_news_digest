CSE surges past 23,500 mark as Fitch praises budget

Trading activity on the Colombo Stock Exchange (CSE) was bullish yesterday, driven by a positive assessment of the governmentâ€™s 2026 budget from Fitch Ratings, market analysts said. The rating agency indicated that the authorities remain committed to reducing the government debt-to-GDP ratio over the medium term, having already met their debt sustainability targets in the 2025 budget.

Due to positive market performance, favourable macroeconomic fundamentals and satisfactory quarterly results also contributed, All Share Price Index surpassed 23,500 mark for the first time.

Amid those developments both indices moved upwards. All Share Price Index up by 77.54 points while S and P SL20 up by 24.27 points. Turnover stood at Rs 7.23 billion with fourteen crossings.

Top seven companies that have mainly contributed to the turnover following the crossings were VallibelOne 150,000 shares crossed to the tune of Rs 82.5 million and its share price traded at 110, NDB 500,000 shares crossed to the tune of Rs 78 million and its share price traded at Rs 156, Sunshine Holdings two million shares crossed to the tune of Rs 74 million and its share price traded at Rs 37, LOLC Holdings 120,000 shares crossed to the tune of Rs 66.6 million and its share price traded at Rs 555, HNB (Non-Voting) 200,000 shares crossed to the tune of Rs 66.3 million and its share price traded at Rs 332, Ceylinco Holdings 15500 shares crossed to the tune of Rs 54.5 million and its share price traded at Rs 3300 and RIL Properties 1.1 million shares crossed to the tune of Rs 41.1 million and its share price traded at Rs 37.40.

In the retail market top seven companies that have mainly contributed to the turnover were DFCC Rs 434 million (2.6 million shares traded) RIL Properties Rs 431 million (11.4 million shares traded), JKH Rs 353 million (15.5 million shares traded), Richard Pieris Rs 340 million (7.4 million shares traded), VallibelOne Rs 312 million (2.8 million shares traded), HNB (Non-Voting) Rs 185 million (555,395 shares traded) and Ambeon Capital Rs 173 million (3.58 million shares traded). During the day 190 million shares volumes changed hands in 45456 transactions.

It was said that banking and finance sector led the market especially DFCC while manufacturing sector especially JKH performed well.

Yesterday the Central Bank announced the US Dollar rate as against rupee. The rupee opened at Rs 304.50/60 to the US dollar in the spot market from Rs 304.20/35 the previous day, dealers said, while bond yields were steady.
