US-Iran peace accord lifts CSE sentiment; turnover hits Rs 3.1bn

The Colombo Stock Exchange (CSE) staged a modest recovery in yesterday’s trading session, buoyed by a positive external sector macroeconomic factor, most notably the recently announced US-Iran peace accord. Analysts believe this geopolitical development has injected a dose of optimism into global markets, and Sri Lanka’s bourse was quick to reflect this improved sentiment.

Both key indices advanced, with the All Share Price Index climbing 50.23 points and the more liquid S&P SL20 adding 20.18 points, while total turnover reached a respectable Rs 3.12 billion, supported by twelve off-market crossings.

Activity was concentrated in select large-cap names. The crossing board saw significant blocks traded in Maharaja Foods, NDB, Digital Mobility Solutions, ACL Cables, Melstacorp, Commercial Bank, and Chevron Lubricants. In the retail segment, Digital Mobility Solutions led turnover, followed closely by Dialog Axiata, while Lanak Realities, ACL Cables, HNB, Aitken Spence, and LB Finance also made notable contributions. Sectorally, the banking and finance space performed well, alongside food and beverage counters, with Maharaja Foods emerging as a standout performer.

On the corporate front, Softlogic Life Insurance announced a strategic regional expansion, entering into a share purchase agreement to acquire a 60% stake in a Dhaka-based life insurer for US$ 1.9 million, marking its foray into the Bangladeshi market. In contrast, trading in Hela Apparel Holdings was suspended effective June 18, 2026, following the company’s failure to rectify a disclaimer of audit opinion in its annual report for the year ended March 2025 – a regulatory compliance issue that dampened sentiment around the stock.

While the day ended with mixed market reactions, the underlying tone driven by the geopolitical relief was quite evident during trading.
