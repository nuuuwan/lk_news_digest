Flood disaster taking toll on share market

The CSE was trading down yesterday shortly after open, as the full extent of the disaster following Cyclone Ditwah was becoming more apparent.

The reason being that investors were concerned that economic development will slow down and cost of production also would increase.

The All Share Price Index was down by 690.77 points, while the S&P SL20 declined by 162.65 points.

“There is some panic selling, which we have seen in the past, too, when starting trading.

The selling was seen across the board. But the market has recovered a bit from open, analysts said.

WindForce said its wholly owned subsidiary, Diya Janani (Pvt) Ltd, has entered into a standard power purchase agreement (SPPA) with the Ceylon Electricity Board to build a 6.5MWdc/5MWac floating solar power project on the Dambuluoya Reservoir. The project is estimated to cost Rs 1,176 million..

People’s Leasing & Finance PLC said it will issue up to 150 million debentures to raise a total of Rs 15 billion.

The listed, rated, unsecured, subordinated, redeemable debentures will be issued at Rs 100 rupees each, and carry a term of five years.

The issue is approved by the Central Bank, and will be subject to required regulatory approvals.
