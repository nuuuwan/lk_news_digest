Bourse indices plunge as oil hits the USD 112 per barrel mark

The CSE yesterday opened down with oil prices hitting the US $112 per barrel mark.

The rise in oil prices followed attacks on energy infrastructure, as Iran conducted missile strikes on the world’s largest LNG export plant in Qatar, following Israel’s strike on Iran’s South Pars gas field, market analysts said.

The All Share Price Index went down 376 points, while the S and P SL20 declined by 117.2 points.

Turnover stood at Rs 3.9 billion with 10 crossings. Top seven crossings were reported in JKH, where 11 million shares crossed to the tune of Rs 261.3 million and its shares traded at Rs 18.30, NTB 546,000 shares crossed for Rs 148 million; its shares traded at Rs 270,Commercial Bank 650,000 shares crossed for Rs 123 million; its shares traded at Rs 190.

Haycarb 656,000 shares crossed for Rs 69.5 million and its shares traded at Rs 107, Colombo Dockyard 500,000 shares crossed to the tune of Rs 59.1 million; its shares traded at Rs 118.25, Sampath Bank 300,000 shares crossed for Rs 44.1 million; its shares sold at Rs 147 and Ambeon Capital 750,000 shares crossed for Rs 25.1 million; its shares traded at Rs 33.50.

In the retail market companies that mainly contributed to the turnover were; JKH Rs 474 million (26.1 million shares traded), HNB Rs 179 million (455,000 shares traded), Softlogic Capital Rs 166 million (12.2 million shares traded), Commercial Bank Rs 128 million (670,000 shares traded), Sampath Bank Rs 115 million (783,000 shares traded), Prime Lands Residencies Rs 114 million (2.6 million shares traded) and HNB Finance Rs 100.7 million (7.7 million shares traded). During the day 176 million share volumes changed hands in 35000 transactions.

JKH performed well while banking the sector, especially NTB, Sampath and Commercial Bank performed well. In the real estate sector Prime Lands performed well.

Yesterday the rupee was quoted at Rs 311.25/40 to the US dollar in the spot market , from Rs 311.30/50 the previous day, dealers said, while bond yields were quoted and traded up.
