ComBank, CBA launch smart POS for SMEs

Commercial Bank of Ceylon, in partnership with Ceylon Business Appliances (CBA), has introduced the ‘PAX E700,’ a new Android-powered smart Point-of-Sale (POS) device, aimed at empowering small and medium enterprises (SMEs) with advanced digital payment solutions.

The all-in-one device is designed to streamline operations, digitize transactions, and enhance the customer checkout experience by combining a traditional cash register with a modern payment terminal. It supports multiple payment methods, including Visa, MasterCard, UnionPay, and LankaPay, and offers connectivity via Wi-Fi, 4G, and LAN.

S. Ganeshan, Deputy General Manager – Personal Banking at Commercial Bank, stated the launch aligns with the bank’s commitment to the SME sector. “We are providing a cutting-edge digital tool that can play a pivotal role in helping businesses grow and thrive,” he said, noting the device helps reduce operational costs and improve sales efficiency.

Ruwath Fernando, CEO of CBA, highlighted the partnership’s role in advancing payment solutions in Sri Lanka. “We’re excited to see how this innovative product will empower businesses to offer secure, efficient, and convenient payment services,” he said. He added that the collaboration leverages Commercial Bank’s financial services and CBA’s technology expertise to support the country’s digital transformation.

Commercial Bank, the largest private sector lender and the biggest provider of credit to SMEs in Sri Lanka, serves as the acquiring partner for the new system.
