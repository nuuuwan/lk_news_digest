Sri Lanka reassures tourists: Island remains safe and open amidst weather conditions

In an official statement released on November 27, 2025, the Sri Lanka Tourism Development Authority (SLTDA) has moved to reassure all visitors that the country remains fully open and safe for tourism, despite the prevailing seasonal rainfall in some parts of the island.

The authority emphasized that the situation is being closely monitored in coordination with national authorities and that comprehensive measures are in place to ensure the safety and comfort of all travelers.

“We wish to reassure all tourists that Sri Lanka remains safe and open for travel and tourism,” the statement read. “Our major tourist destinations, including hotels, transport services, and key attractions, are operating as usual without disruption.”

To assist visitors, the SLTDA has confirmed that its tourism partners are working alongside local authorities to maintain seamless operations. The authority also highlighted the availability of its 24-hour tourist hotline for any queries or required assistance.
