JKH sells off Cinnamon Citadel for Rs. 2.8 bn. ahead of opening new Kandy property

John Keells Hotels PLC, a member of the JKH group, has sold its decades owned riverfront Kandy property for nearly Rs. 2.8 billion to Lavendish Leisure, the hospitality arm of the Dedigama Group in the context of commencement of operations their new hotel, Kandy Myst by Cinnamon, in the hill capital.

JKH said in a stock exchange filing that the group was divesting its total shareholding of 98.39% Kandy Walk Inn Ltd. which is the owning company of the Cinnamon Citadel hotel, one of the older properties owned by the group. It will continue, in agreement with the Acquirer, to manage the hotel under the Cinnamon Hotels and Resorts brand for a minimum period of 12 months subject to review thereafter.

Established in 1950, the Dedigama group’s business reach spans financial services, jewellery, gem and jewel exports, hospitality, commercial real estate and property development, plantations and international joint ventures across various verticals.

JKH said”the divestment is aligned with the asset light strategy of the hotels business and the commencement of operations of Kandy Myst by Cinnamon, the operating entity of which is 40% owned by the Company. Further, proceeds from the divestment will facilitate the optimum allocation of capital towards upgrading the hotel portfolio of the Company in line with the positioning and future outlook of these properties,” the JKH filing said.

The transaction is expected to be completed by March 31, 2026.
