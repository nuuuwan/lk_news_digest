CSE trading yesterday was not at its best because of lingering investor worries stemming from the recent cyclone-driven devastation and its aftermath.

However, the S and P SL20 index recovered during the latter part of the session, market analysts said.

The All Share Price Index went up by 49.23 points, while the S and P SL20 rose by 6.15 points. Turnover stood at Rs 3 billion with three crossings.

Those crossings were: HNB 600,000 shares crossed to the tune of 234 billion; its shares traded at Rs 390, Tokyo Cement (Non-Voting) 500,000 shares crossed for Rs 43.6 million; its shares traded at Rs 88 and Sunshine Holdings 1 million shares crossed for Rs 35.7 million; its shares sold at Rs 35.70.

In the retail market top seven companies that mainly contributed to the turnover were; Colombo Dockyard Rs 344.8 million (1.5 million shares traded), Tokyo Cement (Non-Voting) Rs 215.5 million (2.5 million shares traded), Swisstec Rs 144 million (1.5 million shares traded) JKH Rs 130.6 million (6.2 million shares traded), JKH Rs 130.6 million (6.2 million shares traded), Renuka Agri Foods Rs 106 million (10.9 million shares traded), ACL Cables Rs 105 million (410,000 shares traded) and Three Acre Farm Rs 98 million (152,000 shares traded). During the day 70.8 million shares traded in 24659 transactions.

It is said that the manufacturing sector, especially Colombo Dockyard and JKH, performed well while banking sector and Plantation sector counters performed well too.

Yesterday the rupee was quoted at Rs 300.05/15 to the US dollar in the spot market, recovering slightly from Rs 308.15/40 the previous day, dealers said, while bond yields dropped significantly.

The rupee has fallen from 293.10/30 to 308.15/40 over 2025 amid record current account deficits, sharply reduced budget deficits, as people paid more taxes.

In the secondary market yields were down across the board, dealers said.

A bond maturing on 15.02.2028 was quoted at 8.93/98 percent, down quite a bit from 9.05/10 percent.

A bond maturing on 01.05.2028 was quoted at 8.97/9.02 percent.

A bond maturing on 15.12.2029 was quoted at 9.40/45 percent, down from at 9.50/55 percent.

A bond maturing on 01.07.2030 was quoted at 9.56/62 percent.

A bond maturing on 15.03.2031 was quoted at 9.85/90 percent.

A bond maturing on 15.12.2032 was quoted at 10.20/25 percent, down from 10.22/30 percent.

A bond maturing on 01.11.2033 was quoted at 10.35/40 percent, down from 10.35/44 percent.
