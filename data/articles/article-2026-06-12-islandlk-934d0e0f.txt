CSE registers sharp recovery as stock indices spike

CSE yesterday indicated a sharp recovery after some setbacks the previous day due to the active participation of bargain hunters at the floor.

Amid those developments both indices moved upwards. The All Share Price Index went up by 372.93 points while the S and P SL20 moved up by 87.71 points. Turnover stood at Rs 1.99 billion with seven crossings.

Those crossings were; NTB crossed 488,000 shares to the tune of Rs 133.6 million; its shares traded at Rs 305, Digital Mobility Solutions 657,000 shares crossed for Rs 104.5 million; its shares traded at Rs 159, Prime Lands Residencies 2.3 million shares crossed for Rs 92 million; its shares sold at Rs 40, Commercial Bank 300,000 shares crossed for Rs 60 million; its shares traded at Rs 200, CIC Holdings 900,000 shares crossed for Rs 27 million; its shares sold at Rs 30.30, CCS 174,000 shares crossed for Rs 23.8 million; its shares traded at Rs 136.75 and Dialog Axiata 500,000 shares crossed for Rs 21.5 million; its shares sold at Rs 43.

In the retail market companies that mainly contributed to the turnover were; NDB Rs 106 million (436,000 shares traded), Dialog Axiata Rs 70 million (1.6 million shares traded), JKH Rs 63 million (3.2 million shares traded), Softlogic Capital Rs 51 million (4.1 million shares traded), LMF Rs 51 million (599,000 shares traded), Sierra Cables Rs 48 million (1.7 million shares traded) and CIC (Non Voting) Rs 43 million (1.7 million shares traded). During the day 83.8 million shares changed hands in 19910 transactions.

It is said that banking sector counters, especially NTB and Commercial Bank, performed well while the manufacturing sector, especially JKH, performed well. The real estate sector stocks, especially Prime Lands Residencies, also performed satisfactorily.

Colombo Fort Investments announced that 85,958 ordinary voting shares of the company have been listed with effect from June 12, 2026, pursuant to a scrip dividend. Shares were down 1.96 percent at Rs.375.00.

Meanwhile, Colombo Investment Trust noted that 62,028 ordinary voting shares of the company have also been listed effective June 12, 2026, following a scrip dividend. Stocks were trading down 2.84 percent at Rs.394.00.

Yesterday the rupee was quoted at Rs 335.50/336.00 to the US dollar in the spot market from Rs 336.00/337.00 the previous day, dealers said, while bond yields rallied on optimism around a US-Iran deal.

Activity increased in the secondary market, with bond yields quoted and trading significantly lower.
