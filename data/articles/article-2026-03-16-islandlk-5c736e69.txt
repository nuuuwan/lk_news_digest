Heavy entertainment … by way of rockets and missiles!

The war is taking its toll on the entertainment scene, as well, especially in the Gulf States.

Right now, hardly any night spots are active and that is because of Ramadan.

“All our music shows are stopped for this month, in view of Ramadan. Yes, there was also an attack here, but we are staying safe,” said Niluk Uswaththa, a Sri Lankan musician, with the Seven Notes band, based in Dubai.

In Doha. I was told, by another Sri Lankan musician, Tiron Ismet, that there is heavy entertainment … by way of rockets and missiles by Iran!

No doubt, entertainment in the Gulf States will be experiencing disruptions, due to the ongoing conflict, until normalcy returns.

Major events, concerts, and shows across the region are facing disruptions.

While there’s no explicit confirmation that entertainment is at a standstill, in the Gulf region, it’s possible that some events and attractions will be affected, with cancellations and postponements likely.

Artistes like American singer, songwriter and rapper Kesha, and world-renowned Belgian DJ Charlotte de Witte have cancelled performances due to travel-related issues and airspace closures.

However, according to reports coming my way, some nightspots in the Gulf region are likely to operate, after the Ramadan, with adjustments to their programmes.
