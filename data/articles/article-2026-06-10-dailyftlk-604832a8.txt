Cabinet nod to submit digital asset declaration rules for Parliament approval

Cabinet Spokesman says OSA will likely get approval at next week’s meeting

The Cabinet of Ministers at their meeting on Monday approved submitting newly gazetted 2026 anti-corruption regulations, which mandate digital asset declarations and target high-risk officials, to Parliament for approval.

On 30 March 2026, the Cabinet of Ministers approved the publication of the Anti-Corruption (Declaration of Assets and Liabilities through Centralised Electronic System) Regulations, 2026 and the Anti-Corruption (High Risk Groups to Submit Declaration of Assets and Liabilities) Regulations, 2026, made in accordance with the provisions of the Anti-Corruption Act, No. 9 of 2023, through a Gazette Notification.

The said regulations were also published through Extraordinary Gazette Notifications Nos. 2482/03 and 2482/04 dated on 30 March 2026, respectively.

The proposal to this effect was submitted by Justice and National Integration Minister Harshana Nanayakkara.

Responding to a question posed by a journalist on progress of amendments to the Online Safety Act (OSA), Cabinet Spokesman and Minister Dr. Nalinda Jayatissa expressed hope that it will receive Cabinet approval at the next meeting.
