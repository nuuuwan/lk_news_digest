Sri Lanka deports 125 Chinese nationals over online financial fraud

A group of 125 Chinese nationals detained in Sri Lanka for involvement in online financial fraud has been deported, the Department of Immigration and Emigration said.

The Department said the deportation was carried out using a special aircraft sent from China following discussions between the two Governments. The individuals had been arrested on 17 March in Anuradhapura, where they were found staying in several lodges and engaging in internet-based financial scams.

Investigations revealed that the group had entered Sri Lanka on tourist and business visas but remained in violation of immigration laws. They were subsequently held at the Welisara Detention Centre until arrangements were made for deportation.

A team of officials from China’s Immigration Department, accompanied by a doctor, arrived in Sri Lanka to oversee the process. The deportation took place Sunday night, with six officials from Sri Lanka’s Immigration Department also travelling to China alongside the group.
