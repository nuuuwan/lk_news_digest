Limo Plantation pioneers sustainable agribusiness drive

Limo Plantation is emerging as a key player in transforming Sri Lanka’s agricultural sector, championing innovation, sustainability and export-oriented growth under its guiding vision “Ekwa Wawamu – Rata Dinawamu” (Let’s Plant Together to Win the Nation).

The company, which has earned GAP (Good Agricultural Practices) certification, is recognised for its adherence to international food safety standards, environmental stewardship and quality assurance. It also works closely with the Department of Export Agriculture to expand export-driven agricultural initiatives countrywide.

A major focus of its expansion is the Dolukanda Estate project, where high-value crops such as guava, cinnamon and vanilla are cultivated for long-term commercial viability. The company is also preparing to scale up pepper cultivation and develop sandalwood plantations aimed at strengthening Sri Lanka’s position in global spice and timber markets.

In Chilaw, the Bangadeniya Hi-Tech Farm has expanded from four to sixteen greenhouses, incorporating hydroponic systems and advanced crop monitoring technologies. Nearly 80 percent of its water requirements are met through harvested rainwater, underscoring its commitment to sustainable farming practices. The facility currently produces a range of high-quality crops, including lettuce varieties, salad cucumber, tomato, chilli and cantaloupe melon, targeting both local and export markets.

Beyond commercial farming, Limo Plantation says it aims to promote a broader “agrarian nation” concept by encouraging public participation in modern agriculture, creating employment opportunities and fostering awareness of sustainable food production.

The company says its rapid growth reflects the commitment of its management and skilled workforce as it positions itself as a model for modern agribusiness development in Sri Lanka.
