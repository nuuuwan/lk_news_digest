HP Outlines Five-Pillar Strategy to Enable Digital Inclusion in Sri Lanka

HP has reinforced its long-term commitment to Sri Lanka’s digital transformation, unveiling a five-pillar strategic framework focused on expanding access and accelerating AI-led growth across the country.

At a media roundtable led by Ipsita Dasgupta, SVP and MD for HP India, Sri Lanka, and Bangladesh, the company highlighted Sri Lanka as one of its fastest-growing markets in the region, with approximately 27% market share as of Q4 2025.

The first pillar aims to put a PC in every child’s hand, bridging the digital divide through AI-driven, language-enabled devices for personalized learning. The second focuses on empowering MSMEs through on-device AI to improve efficiency, security, and productivity.

HP’s third pillar involves partnering with local enterprises on technology modernization and workforce strategies. The fourth explores government collaborations in healthcare, education, and skilling through proof-of-concept initiatives.

The fifth pillar positions emerging markets at the centre of HP’s growth strategy, developing tailored solutions to unlock the next wave of digital users.

“Sri Lanka reflects the ambition, resilience, and digital potential we see across emerging economies,” said Dasgupta, reaffirming HP’s commitment to enabling inclusive growth through technology access and innovation.
