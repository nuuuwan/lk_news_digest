Power cuts Imposed to maintain grid stability: Minister

Electricity supply was temporarily disconnected in nearly 100 areas, including parts of Colombo, due to an imbalance in the national power grid, Power and Energy Minister Anura Karunathilaka said.

The Minister explained that the measure was taken to ensure the stability of the electricity system after power generation exceeded demand as a result of higher-than-anticipated solar power generation.

He noted that recent rainy weather conditions had led authorities to expect lower levels of solar generation. However, clear skies and strong sunlight today resulted in a significant amount of electricity being generated through rooftop solar systems and fed into the national grid.

According to the Minister, the situation has primarily affected areas in the Western Province, where rooftop solar installations are concentrated, including Kalutara and Panadura.

He further stated that the temporary power interruptions were implemented as a precautionary measure to prevent the risk of a major islandwide blackout.

The Minister added that electricity supply is expected to be fully restored between 2.00 p.m. and 3.00 p.m. today.
