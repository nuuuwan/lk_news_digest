Profit-takings derail bourse’s bullish run but investor optimism prevails

CSE trading was extremely bullish at first but later indicated mixed reactions due to profit- takings during the latter part of the session.

However, the investors are optimistic about satisfactory quarterly corporate results, which so far indicate good profits in many companies.

The All Share Price Index went up by 59.88 points, while the S and P SL20 declined by 7.32 points. Turnover stood at Rs 9.2 billion with 13 crossings.

Top seven crossings were reported in DFCC where five million shares crossed to the tune of Rs 824 million and its shares traded at Rs 163, Laughs Gas 11 million shares crossed for Rs 651 million; its shares traded at Rs 61, Royal Ceramics 7.5 million shares crossed for Rs 353 million; its shares traded at Rs 47.20, Dipped Products 3.1 million shares crossed to the tune of Rs 216 million; its shares traded at Rs 70, Laugfs Power 10 million shares crossed for Rs 150 million; its shares traded at Rs 15, Cargills 100,000 shares crossed for Rs 81 million; its shares traded at Rs 805 and Colombo Dockyard 370,000 shares crossed for Rs 56 million; its shares traded at Rs 180.

In the retail market seven companies that mainly contributed to the turnover were; Laugfs Gas Rs 423 million (seven million shares traded), Overseas Realty Rs 232 million (5.5 million shares traded), Ambeon Capital Rs 220 (4.4 million shares traded), LOLC Finance Rs 196 million (31 million shares traded), Cargills Bank Rs 195 million (18 million shares traded), Colombo Dockyards Rs 178 million (957,000 shares traded) and Hayleys Rs 177 million (897,000 shares traded). During the day 307 million shares volumes changed hands in 46800 transactions.

Yesterday the rupee opened at Rs 303.58/63 to the US dollar, weaker from Rs 303.40/50 the previous day, while bond yields were broadly steady, dealers said.
