Metropolitan & Canon debut ‘groundbreaking’ cinema tech with local short film ‘Little Life’

Metropolitan Technologies has marked a pivotal moment for Sri Lankan cinema by introducing advanced digital cinematography technology through the theatrical premiere of the short film Little Life.

The film, directed by acclaimed cinematographer Roshan Fernando, was shot exclusively using Canon’s new Cinema EOS C400. Its screening served as the first large-format demonstration in Sri Lanka of the camera’s dynamic range, colour science, and low-light capabilities, signaling Canon and Metropolitan’s active entry into the local film industry.

“It is a proud moment for Canon and Metropolitan as we step into the next phase of our five-decade partnership,” said Taslim Rahaman, Director of Metropolitan Technologies.

Little Life delivers a poignant narrative centred on the street dogs of Colombo, exploring themes of compassion and urban coexistence. Fernando, whose credits span Netflix, National Geographic, and live productions for artists like Enrique Iglesias, noted the unique creative challenge of capturing the story. “The EOS C400 enabled us to convey emotion visually,” he said.

The event was attended by leading filmmakers, including renowned filmmaker Chandran Rutnam, who emphasized that cinema is driven by emotional impact. The launch underscores Metropolitan’s commitment, as the official Canon distributor, to empowering Sri Lankan creators with world-class technology.
