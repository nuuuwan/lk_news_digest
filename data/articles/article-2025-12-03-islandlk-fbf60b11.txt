CSE continues in panic-selling mode as investor fears persist

The CSE indicated some panic-selling yesterday as well, as investors seemed to agonize over lingering economic uncertainties coming in the wake of the flood havoc, market analysts said.

The All Share Price Index went down by 356.4 points, while the S and P SL20 went down by 97.84 points. Turnover stood at Rs 3.7 billion with six crossings.

Those crossings were reported in JKH, where 11 million shares crossed to the tune of Rs 236.5 million; its shares traded at Rs 21.50, Hemas Holdings three million shares crossed to the tune of Rs 106 million; its shares sold at Rs 35, CIC Holdings 1 million shares crossed for Rs 32.5 million; its shares traded at Rs 32.50, ACL Cables 100,000 shares crossed for Rs 24 million; its shares traded at Rs 240, Commercial Bank 120,000 shares crossed for Rs 23.9 million; its shares traded at Rs 198 and Access Engineering 300,000 shares crossed for Rs 21.3 million; its shares sold at Rs 71.

In the retail market companies that mainly contributed to the turnover were; Access Engineering Rs 282 million (3.98 million shares traded), Hemas Holdings Rs 150 million (4.3 million shares traded), JKH Rs 125.5 million (5.8 million shares traded), Colombo Dockyards Rs 118 million (286,000 shares traded), HNB Rs 114 million (291,000 shares traded), Tokyo Cement (Non-Voting) Rs 100 million (1.4 million shares traded) and Sierra Cables Rs 94 million (2.9 million shares traded). During the day 171 million share volumes changed hands in 37,367 transactions.

It is said that manufacturing sector counters, especially JKH and Hemas Holdings, contributed mainly to the market while the banking sector, especially Commercial Bank, was active in the market too. Further, construction sector counters, especially Access Engineering, contributed to the turnover.

Yesterday, the rupee was quoted at 308.85/309.00 to the US dollar in the spot market, weaker from 308.80/90 on Tuesday, dealers said, while bond yields recovered on the shorter tenors and were somewhat steady on the longer tenors.

A bond maturing on 15.03.2028 was quoted at 9.18/20 percent, down from 9.20/25 percent.

A bond maturing on 15.06.2029 was quoted at 9.53/58 percent.

A bond maturing on 01.07.2030 was quoted flat at 9.65/75 percent.

A bond maturing on 15.03.2031 was quoted at 9.95/10.10 percent.

A bond maturing on 15.12.2032 was quoted at 10.35/40 percent, down from 10.35/45 percent.
