ACME Printing and Packaging shares up 3.33 percent as deal with Singer (Sri Lanka) takes shape

Singer (Sri Lanka) has entered into a sale and purchase agreement with ACME Printing and Packaging to buy its factory in Piliyandala for Rs 630 million.

The move is “part of the restructuring of the Company and the expansion of the factory premises at Pannala,” ACME said in a market disclosure, CSE sources said.

The factory is situated at 345, at Gonamadiththa Road, Piliyandala.

Shares of ACME Printing and Packaging were trading at Rs.6.20, up 3.33 percent. Singer shares were up 60 cents at Rs 87.50.

Amid those developments, CSE yesterday kicked off on a positive note following a Janashakthi IPO.

Therefore both indices showed an upward trend. All Share Price Index went up by 17.94 points while S and P SL20 rose by 3.72 points.

Turnover stood at Rs 3.72 billion with seven crossings. Those crossings were; Digital Solutions 1.1 million shares crossed to the tune of Rs 172.4 million; its shares traded at Rs 152, Prime Lands Residencies 3 million shares crossed to the tune of Rs 146.7 million; its shares traded at Rs 49, NDB 1 million shares crossed for Rs 112 million; its shares sold at Rs 112, Hemas Holdings 1.7 million shares crossed for Rs 56.1 million; its shares traded at Rs 33, Sunshine Holdings 1.1 million shares crossed for Rs 35.5 million; its shares sold at Rs 32, Vallibel Finance 360,000 shares crossed for Rs 229.4 million; its shares traded at Rs 98 and Access Engineering 300,000 shares crossed to the tune of 22.8 million; its shares sold at Rs 76.

In the retail market top seven companies that mainly contributed to the turnover were; Janashakthi Rs 1.79 billion (124.9 million shares traded), NDB Rs 127 million (1.1 million shares traded), JKH Rs 114.1 million (5.5 million shares traded), Asiri Hospital RS 99.4 million (2.4 million shares traded), ACL Cables Rs 80.9 million (797,000 shares traded), CCS Rs 73 million (597,800 shares traded) and Acme Printers Rs 72.3 million (11.3 million shares traded). During the day 214.4 million share volumes changed hands in 43852 transactions.

It is said that Janashakthi led the market which contributed one third of the turnover, while the banking sector, especially NDB, performed well. The Real estate sector, especially Prime Lands Residencies, performed well. Further, manufacturing sector counters also performed significantly well.

Yesterday the rupee was quoted at Rs 319.50/320.50 to the US dollar in the spot next market, from Rs 319.00/320.00 the previous day, dealers said, while bond yields were steady.
