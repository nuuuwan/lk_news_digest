Selling pressure moves yields higher in secondary market

The secondary market witnessed some selling pressure amidst moderate volumes and limited market activity during yesterday’s session.

The yield curve adjusted up, especially driven by medium to long-term yields.

Among the traded maturities, 01.08.2030 traded between 11.90 percent to 12.10 percent. Moving ahead, 15.01.2033 and 01.06.2033 changed hands at 12.30 percent and 12.75 percent, respectively. Moreover, 15.03.2035 was traded within the range of 12.95 percent and 13.05 percent.

On the external front, the Sri Lankan rupee depreciated against the US dollar, standing at Rs.329.22/US dollar, compared to Rs.327.82/US dollar seen earlier.

The overnight liquidity in the banking system expanded to Rs.102.46 billion, from Rs.92.15 billion recorded previously.
