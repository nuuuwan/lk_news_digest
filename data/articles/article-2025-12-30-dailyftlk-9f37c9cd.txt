GREE Airtech opens flagship store in Nawala

Gree has marked a significant milestone in its Sri Lankan journey with the opening of its flagship store at No. 378, Araliya Gardens, Nawala Road, Nawala, on 22 December 2025.

The flagship store was officially opened by Gree Airtech Ltd., the authorised agent for GREE in Sri Lanka.

The new flagship showroom reflects GREE’s long-term commitment to the local market and has been designed as a comprehensive brand experience. It provides customers, industry professionals, and partners with the opportunity to engage with GREE’s technology, design philosophy, and service standards within a modern, purpose-built retail environment.

Coinciding with the opening, Gree Airtech Ltd., also introduced two new air-conditioning models to the Sri Lankan market. The GREE Joywind Floor Standing Inverter Air Conditioner is designed for large residential and commercial spaces, offering powerful and efficient climate control for environments that require consistent performance across wider areas. The GREE Clivia Air Conditioner, offered in four distinctive colours—Champagne Gold, Navy Blue, Black, and Silver—blends refined aesthetics with advanced functionality, featuring AI-driven climate optimisation, UV sterilisation, and multiple smart controls designed to elevate everyday living and wellness.

GREE Airtech Ltd., Managing Director Adhil Anees said: “The opening of our flagship store in Nawala marks an important step in strengthening the GREE brand in Sri Lanka. It reflects our long-term vision to build a strong market presence supported by quality products, reliable service, and meaningful customer engagement.”

The opening ceremony was attended by company leadership, industry stakeholders, dealers, and invited guests, highlighting the strategic importance of the occasion.

With the launch of the Nawala flagship store and the expansion of its product portfolio, GREE Airtech Ltd., continues to reinforce GREE’s position as a trusted provider of innovative and reliable air-conditioning solutions in Sri Lanka.
