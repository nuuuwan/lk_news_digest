Banking sector counters keep bourse in robust health

The CSE experienced a measure of robustness yesterday due to a satisfactory performance by banking sector counters triggered by the latest quarterly results. The All Share Price Index went up by 78.13 points, while the S and P SL20 rose by 27.17 points. Turnover stood at Rs 4.9 billion with 12 crossings.

Topmost seven crossings were reported in Sampath Bank, where 950,000 shares crossed to the tune of Rs 152.5 million; its shares traded at Rs 150.70, Colombo Dockyards 500,000 shares crossed to the tune of Rs 75.5 million; its shares traded at Rs 151, Access Engineering 802000 shares crossed for Rs 54.2 million; its shares sold at Rs 80, EB Creasy one million shares crossed to the tune of Rs 61 million; its shares traded at Rs 61, LOLC Holdings 100,000 shares crossed for Rs 57.3 million; its shares traded at Rs 573.50, Pan Asia Power 2.4 million shares crossed for Rs 52.4 million; its shares sold at Rs 22 and Hemas Holdings 1.5 million shares crossed for Rs 49 million; its shares traded at Rs 33.50.

In the retail market, top seven companies that mainly contributed to the turnover were; Softlogic Capital Rs 360 million (33.9 million shares traded), Access Engineering Rs 308 million (3.6 million shares traded), LMF Rs 283 million (3.1 million shares traded), Colombo Land and Developments Rs 232.6 million (four million shares traded) Prime Lands Residencies Rs 198 million (3.3 million shares traded), JKH Rs 192 million (8.4 million shares traded) and HNB Rs 153 million (36000 shares traded). During the day 195 million share volumes changed hands in 38496 transactions.

Prime Lands Residencies said it has entered into a 99-year lease agreement with Sri Lanka’s Urban Development Authority (UDA) for a mixed development project on a land at Bauddhaloka Mawatha, Colombo 7, where the Otters Aquatic Club is presently operating.

“The proposed lease arrangement has been structured to accommodate the relocation of the existing Otters Aquatic Club facilities within the planned mixed-use development of Prime Lands, CSE sources reported.

The banking sector led the market, especially Sampath Bank. Manufacturing sector also performed well, especially JKH and EB Creasy. Further, the real estate sector also performed well, especially Prime Lands Residencies and Access Engineering.

Yesterday the rupee was quoted at Rs 309.33/37 to the US dollar in the spot market, dealers said, while bond yields were broadly steady.
