Principal CSE indices do a dip; regional markets too finish low

The CSE closed down yesterday with the benchmark All Share Price Index moving down 0.75 percent.

The ASPI was down 166.55 points at 22,011.10, while the more liquid S&P SL20 was down 0.54 percent, or 32.82 points, at 6,097.83.

Positive contributors to the ASPI were JKH (up 0.50 percent at Rs 20.10 ), Lion Brewery Ceylon (up 2.85 percent at Rs 1,849.00 ), and Seylan Developments (up 11.11 percent at Rs 1.00 ).

Dialog Axiata (down 2.51 percent at Rs 42.80 ), Sampath Bank (down 0.88 percent at Rs 140.75 ), Ceylon Beverage Holdings (down 6.28 percent at Rs 2,750.75 ), and Ceylon Cold Stores (down 3.22 percent at Rs 135.25) were top negative contributors.

Market turnover was Rs 2.05 billion . It is said that Consumer Durables & Apparels led the turnover with Rs 413.9 million .

Analysts noted that regional markets also finished lower, with Hong Kong’s Hang Seng index down 1.56 percent at 25,633.21 and India’s Nifty 50 dropping 0.33 percent to 23,405.60.

Meanwhile, Maharaja Foods announced the opening of its newest retail outlet, “Maharaja Super – SPAR Savemore” in Kotahena on June 1.

Yesterday the rupee was quoted at Rs 333.00/335.50 to the US dollar in the spot market on, weaker from Rs 332.00/333.50 the previous day, dealers said, while bond yields were broadly steady.

The telegraphic transfer rate for Sri Lanka’s rupee against the US dollar was 328.50 buying, 337.50 selling.
