Sri Lanka remains home

Roger Menezes, a name that instantly brings back the soundtrack of Sri Lanka’s 1960s and 70s music scene, is in town – the second for this year – and that’s because he considers Sri Lanka his second home.

He moved to Sydney in 1973, where he formed Eastern Spice and later Stay Tuned.

Despite his life abroad, Sri Lanka remains home, and Roger still returns regularly to perform for music lovers who grew up on his music.

Roger emphasised the purpose of this particular trip was mainly to surprise a very special fan (close to his heart, he says), who celebrated her birthday on 22nd May.

However, he also went into action at a venue, in Dehiwela, and on Sunday, 07th June, he will be featured at Jazz Sunday.

Roger goes back to Sydney, on 14th June, but will be in Sri Lanka again, towards the latter part of September, and will be seen in action at a new venue, away from Colombo.

With a career spanning over five decades, Roger played drums, guitar, keyboards, and saxophone alongside legendary bands like The Jetliners, The Savages, Thunderbirds, and Fireflies, before fronting his own group, Roger and the Webs.
