IRCSL marks 25 years of transforming Sri Lanka’s insurance sector

The Insurance Regulatory Commission of Sri Lanka (IRCSL) is celebrating its 25th anniversary, commemorating a journey from a basic supervisory body to a modern, data-driven regulator.

Established under the Regulation of Insurance Industry Act No. 43 of 2000, the IRCSL has played a pivotal role in enhancing public trust and fostering sound risk management practices across the nation.

At a press conference marking the milestone, Director General Damayanthi Fernando highlighted the shift from a rule-based framework to a robust risk-based system, including the implementation of Risk-Based Capital models.

Chairman Dr. Ajith Raveendra De Mel emphasized strategic reforms and staff development to meet future challenges, unveiling initiatives like the Digital Motor Insurance Card and a Centralized Motor Insurance Data Repository to enhance transparency.

Looking ahead, the IRCSL will host an OECD/ADBI Roundtable in June and launch a roadmap targeting doubled insurance penetration by 2030, reinforcing its commitment to policyholder protection and a financially resilient Sri Lanka.
