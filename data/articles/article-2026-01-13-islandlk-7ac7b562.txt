Bourse sets off on initial negative note but bounces back

CSE activities kicked off on a slightly negative note yesterday due to an increased Treasury Bond rate announced the previous day but later the market turned positive, market analysts said.

Amid those developments both indices moved upwards. The All-Share Price Index went up by 26.35 points, while the S and P SL20 rose by 5.32 points. Turnover stood at Rs 4.94 billion with five crossings.

Those crossings were reported in Citizens Developments Business Finance where 685,000 shares crossed to the tune of Rs 254 million; its shares traded at Rs 370, Sierra Cables one million shares crossed to the tune of Rs 35 million; its shares traded at Rs 35, Hemas Holdings one million shares crossed for Rs 34.7 million; its shares traded at Rs 34.70, Lanka Realty Investments 600,000 shares crossed for Rs 30.6 million; its shares traded at Rs 51 and Tokyo Cement (Non-Voting) 250,000 shares crossed for Rs 23.5 million; its shares sold at Rs 94.

In the retail market top seven companies that mainly contributed to the turnover were: Commercial Bank Rs 250 million (1.1 million shares traded), Sierra Cables Rs 231 million (6.4 million shares traded), RIL Properties Rs 223 million (six million shares traded), Melstacorp Rs 201 million (1.1 million shares traded), Prime Lands Residencies Rs 195 million (five million shares traded), Digital Mobility Solutions Rs 188 million (1 million shares traded) and Lanka Realty Investments Rs 140 million (2.8 million shares traded). During the day 133 million share volumes changed hands in 37968 transactions.

It is said that the banking and finance sector led the market, especially Commercial Bank and Citizens Developments Business Finance, while construction related companies, especially Tokyo Cement, were also active at the floor.

Yesterday the rupee was quoted at Rs 309.05/15 to the US dollar in the spot market, weaker from Rs 309.00/10

the previous day, having depreciated in recent weeks, dealers said, while the opening quotes for short tenors were higher.

The telegraphic transfer rates for the American dollar were 305.5000 buying, 312.5000 selling; the British pound was 410.7291 buying, and 422.0473 selling, and the euro was 353.9701 buying, 365.3583 selling.
