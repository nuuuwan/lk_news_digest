Restructuring of SriLankan Airlines’ defaulted bonds has positive impact on CSE

CSE trading overall was positive yesterday having experienced five days of decline. The change for the better came with a Singapore Stock Exchange announcement that SriLankan Airlines had restructured its defaulted bonds with a 15 percent haircut and other conditions.

“We are very pleased to have finally reached an agreement with the Ad Hoc Group of Bondholders, allowing us to now look to the future of our company with greater optimism, chairman, SriLankan Airlines Sarath Ganegoda said in a CSE filing.

Meanwhile, Treasury Secretary Harshana Suriyaperuma announced at a public event that SriLankan will complete the restructuring of 99 percent of its debt with the finalization of an agreement with holders of SriLankan Airlines defaulted bonds.

“Today we are taking a new step in the full normalization of our relations with our external partners and in our efforts to restore our public finances, he said in a filing on the Singapore Stock Exchange after reaching a deal to restructure SriLankan bonds.

Amid those developments both indices moved upwards. The All Share Price Index went up by 75.10 points, while the S and P SL20 moved up by 25.02 points.

Turnover stood at Rs 3.49 billion with ten crossings. Top seven crossings were reported in Commercial Bank, where 950,000 shares crossed to the tune of Rs 198 million; its shares traded at Rs 208.50, JKH 6.68 million shares crossed for Rs 152.3 million; its shares traded at Rs 22.80, VallibelOne 539,933 shares crossed for Rs 56.7 million; its shares traded at Rs 105, Cargills Bank 5.2 million shares crossed for Rs 55 million; its shares sold at Rs 105, Sunshine Holdings one million shares crossed for Rs 35 million; its shares traded at Rs 35 and CTC 15500 shares crossed for Rs 26.5 million and its shares fetched Rs 1700.

In the retail market top seven companies that mainly contributed to the turnover were; JKH Rs 189 million (8.29 million shares traded), CIC Holdings Rs 139 million (4.1 million shares traded), Melstacorp Rs 117 million (640,000 shares traded), Commercial Bank Rs 109 million (524,000 shares traded), Renuka Agri Rs 100 million (11.96 million shares traded) JF Packaging Rs 91.7 million (6.1 million shares traded) and Sampath Bank Rs 73.2 million (493,600 shares traded). During the day 151.2 million shares volumes changed hands in 26373 transactions.

It is said that manufacturing sector, especially JKH, performed well, while the banking sector, especially Commercial Bank, performed well at the floor. Other sectors also performed somewhat well during the day.

Yesterday, the rupee opened at Rs 308.30/50 to the US dollar in the spot market, weaker from Rs 308.10/50 the previous day, dealers said, while bond yields were broadly steady.

The telegraphic transfer rates for the American dollar was 304.5000 buying, 311.5000 selling; the British pound was 396.0242 buying, and 407.3860 selling, and the euro was 348.4579 buying, 359.8211 selling.
