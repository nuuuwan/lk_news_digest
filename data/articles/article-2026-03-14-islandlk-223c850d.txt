One Heart, One Voice – A choral concert presented by the Lanka Alzheimer’s Foundation

Music, friendship and a shared sense of purpose come together this month when the Senior Community Choir of Soul Sounds Academy is featured in “One Heart, One Voice,” a special choral concert presented by the Lanka Alzheimer’s Foundation, directed by Soundarie David Rodrigo. The performance will take place on 22 March 2026 at the Lionel Wendt Theatre, bringing together voices and hearts for a cause that touches countless families.

At Soul Sounds Academy, Monday and Thursday rehearsals are far from quiet affairs. The room hums with animated chatter as choir members gather, greeting one another warmly before settling down to rehearse. Soon the sound of music fills the space as altos, sopranos and descants practise their parts with enthusiasm, building toward what promises to be an uplifting evening of song.

The repertoire is a delightful journey through some of Broadway’s most beloved musicals, featuring numbers from The Sound of Music, My Fair Lady, South Pacific, Mary Poppins, Oklahoma! and Evita, along with several other favourites. Adding to the evening’s appeal will be a guest appearance by Soul Sounds, Sri Lanka’s premier female vocal ensemble.

Presiding over this lively musical gathering is Soundarie David Rodrigo, the Music Director of Soul Sounds Academy. Elegant as always in a beautiful saree, she enters the rehearsal room to warm smiles and eager anticipation. Under her gentle but assured guidance, voices blend and harmonies take shape.

The Senior Community Choir itself is a relatively new initiative. It was founded on 10 March 2025, launched on the birthday of Soundarie’s beloved father. Reflecting on its beginnings, she says: “What began as a small tribute in my father’s memory has become a community filled with music, warmth and companionship—something I know he would have loved”. In the year since its formation, the choir has grown into a close-knit musical family where friendships flourish alongside the music.

Adding a touch of theatrical discipline to rehearsals is  Jerome de Silva, the accomplished Artistic Director. Known for his strict standards and sharp attention to detail, Jerome keeps the choir firmly on track. He is not above delivering a well-aimed reprimand when lyrics falter or focus drifts, but the choir takes his tough love in good humour as they strive to meet the high standards he set.

Beyond the music, “One Heart, One Voice” carries a deeper purpose. The concert coincides with the 25th anniversary of the Lanka Alzheimer’s Foundation, an organisation dedicated to improving the quality of life for those living with Alzheimer’s disease and related dementias, as well as their families and caregivers.

Alzheimer’s disease is a condition that gradually affects memory, thinking and behaviour, and its impact reaches far beyond the individual diagnosed. Many families know the pain of watching a loved one slowly lose memories and independence. Fortunately, greater awareness today has helped replace stigma with compassion and understanding.

The Lanka Alzheimer’s Foundation was founded by Lorraine Yu and Tami Tamitigama, whose vision was to provide support, education and care for families affected by dementia. From modest beginnings, the organisation has grown steadily over the past quarter century.

Today, under the skilful leadership of Professor Shehan Williams, the Foundation continues to expand its work. Its centre in Colombo 10 offers diagnostic services, therapeutic activities and guidance, providing much-needed support to families navigating the challenges of dementia.

Through music and community spirit, the Senior Community Choir hopes to shine a light on this important cause. On the evening of 22 March, their voices will come together in harmony—not only to entertain, but also to inspire compassion and awareness.
