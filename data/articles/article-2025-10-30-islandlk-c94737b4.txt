US Federal Reserve decision seen as impacting local stocks

The CSE was indirectly impacted yesterday following an interest rate amendment by the US Federal Reserve Bank, resulting in both CSE indices reflecting mixed market reactions, analysts said.The All Share Price Index went up by 49.11 points while the S and P SL20 declined by 10.59 points.

Turnover stood at Rs 7.75 billion with 20 crossings. Top seven crossings were: Amana Takaful 44.5 million shares crossed to the tune of Rs 1.2 billion; its shares traded at Rs 29, JKH 19.5 million shares crossed for Rs 401 million; its shares traded at Rs 20.50, Singer Sri Lanka 3.7 million shares crossed to the tune of Rs 348 million; its shares traded at Rs 95, Colombo Dockyards 697,000 shares crossed for Rs 221 million, its shares traded at Rs 30, Haylays 811,000 shares crossed for Rs 158 million; its shares sold at Rs 194.50, Digital Mobility Solutions 934,000 shares crossed for Rs 140 million; its shares sold at Rs 150 and Cargo Boats Development 725,000 shares crossed for Rs 123 million; its shares fetched Rs 170.

In the retail market, the top seven companies that mainly contributed to the turnover were; JKH Rs 371 million (7.9 million shares traded), Colombo Dockyards Rs 346 million (one million shares traded), Digital Mobility Solutions Rs 294 million (1.9 million shares traded), Sierra Cables Rs 228 million (6.8 million shares traded), Amana Takaful Rs 209 million (seven million shares traded), JAT Holdings Rs 120 million (2.3 million shares traded) and Lanka Realty Investments Rs 115 million (three million shares traded). During the day 223 million shares volumes changed hands in 35800 transactions.

It is said that the banking and finance sector led the market; especially Amana Takaful, while the manufacturing sector, especially JKH and Hayleys, performed a pivotal role.

Yesterday the rupee opened around Rs 304.45/55 to the US dollar in the spot market; at around the same level a day earlier at closing time, while bond yields were flat after a fall.The rupee has fallen from Rs 302.55/60 to the US dollar from the beginning of the month.
