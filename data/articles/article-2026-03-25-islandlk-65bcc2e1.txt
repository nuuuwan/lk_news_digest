Trump’s claim on pausing war brings bounce to CSE

The CSE opened on a positive note yesterday after US President Donald Trump claimed he was pausing attacks on Iranian infrastructure.

Consequently, the All Share Price Index touched the 21000 level. It rose by by 750.87 points while S and P SL20 rose by 241.05 points.

Turnover stood at Rs 4.22 billion with six crossings. Those crossings were reported in JKH which crossed 15.3 million shares to the tune of Rs 283.7 million and its shares traded at Rs 18.50, LMF 500,000 shares crossed to the tune of Rs 40 million; its shares sold at Rs 80, Melstacorp 200,000 shares crossed for Rs 33 million; its shares traded at Rs 165, RIL Properties 973,000 shares crossed for Rs 24.7 million; its shares sold at Rs 25.40, Central Finance 100,000 shares crossed to the tune of Rs 22.3 million; its shares sold at Rs 233 and Sampath Bank 140,000 shares crossed for Rs 22 million; its shares fetched at Rs 158.

In the retail market top seven companies that mainly contributed to the turnover were; Softlogic Capital Rs 226 million (15.9 million shares traded), JKH Rs 213 million (11.5 million shares traded) Agri Printing and Packaging Rs 176 million (42 million shares traded), Colombo Dockyard Rs 171 million (1.2 million shares traded), Prime Lands Residencies Rs 155 million (3.2 million shares traded), Sampath Bank Rs 139 million (892,000 shares traded) and HNB (Non-Voting) Rs 136 million (395,00 shares traded). During the day 195.4 million share volumes changed hands in 37097 transactions.

It is said that manufacturing sector counters, especially JKH, led the market, while real estate sector stocks also traded well, especially RIL Properties and Prime Lands Residencies. Banking sector counters almost performed well.

Yesterday the rupee was quoted at Rs 313.50/314.50 to the US dollar in the spot market, depreciating further from 313.00/314.00 the previous day, dealers said, while bond yields recovered slightly with the market a bit volatile.
