Lankan Scout leader Bhanuka Karunaratne first to receive Japan’s Wood Badge

Bhanuka Karunaratne has made scouting history by becoming the first Sri Lankan to be awarded the Wood Badge, the highest international training recognition for Scout leaders, in Japan.

The prestigious honour was formally presented during a Board of Directors meeting of the Scout Association of Japan in Hyogo Prefecture.

The Wood Badge is regarded as one of the most distinguished training awards in the global scouting movement and is conferred on leaders who demonstrate advanced leadership and team-building skills, strong instructional ability, and a deep understanding of scouting principles.

To earn the qualification, Karunaratne completed a rigorous series of theoretical and practical training modules approved by the Scout Association of Japan, designed to develop high-level competencies in scout leadership.

The Wood Badge is an internationally recognised symbol of excellence in scouting, reflecting a commitment to youth development and leadership training.

An alumnus of Kingswood College, Kandy, Karunaratne’s achievement has been hailed as a milestone for Sri Lanka’s scouting movement, bringing recognition to the country on the international stage and serving as an inspiration to scouts across the island.
