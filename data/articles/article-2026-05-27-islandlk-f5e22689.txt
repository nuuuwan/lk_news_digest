Recovery trend catches on in stock trading; field day for bargain hunters

The CSE yesterday indicated a recovery trend due to the realisation of the IMF’s fifth and sixth tranches and saw active participation from bargain hunters who got the opportunity to grab high valued stocks at bargain prices.

Amid those developments both indices moved upwards. The All Share Price Index went up by 24.09 points, while the S and P SL20 rose by 23.39 points. Turnover stood at Rs 1.86 billion with four crossings.

Those crossings were; CIC Holdings which crossed 4.7 million shares crossed to the tune of Rs 157.8 million; its shares traded at Rs 33,LMF 500,000 shares crossed to the tune of Rs 46 million, its shares sold at Rs 92, JKH 2 million shares crossed for Rs 40.2 million and its shares traded at Rs 20.10, Softlogic Life Insurance 225,000 shares crossed for Rs 21 million; its shares sold at Rs 93.50.

In the retail market companies that mainly contributed to the turnover were; Bairaha Farm Rs 131 million (1.4 million shares traded), Dialog Rs 111 million (2.7 million shares traded), Softlogic Life Insurance Rs 106 million (1.1 million shares traded), Vidul Lanka Rs 79.7 million (3.3 million shares traded), JKH Rs 65 million (3.2 million shares traded), Sampath Bank Rs 58.2 million (409,000 shares traded) and LMF Rs 55 million (592,000 shares traded). During the day 51.88 million share volumes changed hands in 19105 transactions.

It is said that manufacturing sector counters, especially JKH and CIC Holdings performed well while consumers sector counters, especially Bahiraha and LMF, performed significantly well.

Yesterday the rupee was quoted at Rs 328.00/330.00 to the US dollar in the spot market on, weaker from Rs 324.00/325.50 the previous day, dealers said, while the secondary bond market was quiet amid the ongoing auction.

The rupee opened at 325/327, weakened to 330/332, and settled back at 327/328 in late morning trade.

An auction of Rs 240,000 million treasury bonds was ongoing.

The telegraphic transfer rate for the Sri Lanka’s rupee against the US dollar was 319.5000 buying, 328.5000 selling, the pound was 428.7514 buying, 442.7970 selling, and the euro 369.3971 buying, 383.3141.
