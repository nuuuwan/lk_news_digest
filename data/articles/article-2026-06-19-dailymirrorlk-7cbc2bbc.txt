Ideal Motors concludes Habarana Abiyogaya 10

Ideal Abiyogaya 10, the 10th phase of the Ideal Abiyogaya community engagement initiative by Ideal Motors, one of Sri Lanka’s leading automotive brands, concluded recently in Habarana with resounding success.

Launched in 2015, the Ideal Abiyogaya programme has been held exclusively for the Mahindra Bolero customers and community. The 10th phase extended across the districts of Anuradhapura, Polonnaruwa, Matale and Trincomalee.   As part of the school‑based industrial training programmes under Ideal Abiyogaya, a Mahindra engine and industrial equipment were donated to the technical department of Habarana Maha Vidyalaya. In parallel, the Ideal Singing Abiyogaya identified talented children from schools in the Kekirawa and Hingurakgoda educational zones, creating future opportunities for the most gifted performers.

The Mahindra Bolero Off‑Road Rally, reserved for Mahindra Bolero customers, was another highlight of the event. The celebrations concluded with a free musical concert that drew a large crowd, underscoring the success and popularity of the programme.   The creator of the Ideal Abiyogaya programme, Ideal Motors Group Founder and Chairman Nalin Welgama shared his thoughts on the journey his company has come so far, the Ideal Abiyogaya programme and Ideal Sewana programmes planned for the future.

“We achieved a major breakthrough when we joined hands with Mahindra in 2009. That advance was made possible by starting a new journey with customers beyond the point of sale. We transformed an era in which everything was centred in Colombo. By delivering strong sales and after‑sales service, Mahindra often invited me to India to gain new technology and experiences, which we then shared with the people of Sri Lanka. One notable initiative was the Mahindra Getaway competition, organised for the Mahindra vehicle users. It provided them with an opportunity to demonstrate the correct use of their vehicles, showcase their quality and highlight their skills.”

He added, “Accordingly, I introduced a new programme in Sri Lanka called the Ideal Abiyogaya. Through this initiative, the Mahindra vehicle users were offered a range of competitions, assessments of their vehicles and the unique opportunity to enjoy a free musical concert by one of the most popular bands in the country, an experience many had never had before.”

Speaking on future plans, he noted, “We aim to introduce several new features to the Ideal Abiyogaya programme. Among them is Ideal Sewana, designed to provide solutions for those facing financial challenges. In addition, the Ideal Abiyogaya initiative will continue to prioritise improvements in health and education. Ideal Motors is also pleased to announce that the 11th phase of Ideal Abiyogaya will be launched soon, focusing on another vibrant region of Sri Lanka.”
