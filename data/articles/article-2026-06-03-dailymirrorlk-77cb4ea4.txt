Meridian Talent counters AI-led hiring with human-first recruitment model

From left: Meridian Creative Solutions funder and Director Viraj Adhihetty and Meridian Talent co-founder and CEO Az Da Rosa

Meridian Talent, the specialist recruitment division of Meridian Creative Solutions, led by Az Da Rosa, argues that hiring accuracy should be treated as a commercial priority, not just a recruitment target.

As hiring becomes increasingly speed-driven and automated, businesses are being pushed to treat hiring as a race to fill roles rather than a decision about long-term fit and performance.

Technology leaders are under mounting pressure to hire quickly but the commercial risk of getting a hire wrong is substantial.

Global research frequently places the cost of a bad hire at 30 percent of first-year salary at minimum, rising materially for senior roles. At the same time, specialised technology roles commonly take weeks to fill, increasing delivery risk, team strain and opportunity cost. Meridian Talent’s model is built to reduce those risks through alignment-first, human-led assessment, where technology supports judgment rather than replacing it.

“AI tools can be useful at handling volume but they cannot reliably measure the things that determine whether someone will thrive in a technical team,” said Meridian Talent co-founder and CEO Az Da Rosa.

“In my experience, the hires that fail are rarely just a skills problem. They’re an alignment problem: working style, communication under pressure, delivery mindset and cultural fit. Our job is to surface that early, so hiring feels less like a gamble and more like a partnership.”

Meridian Creative Solutions founder and Director Viraj Adhihetty said the goal is not to reject automation but to restore context to a process that has become overly keyword-driven.

“Automation and AI absolutely have a place in modern hiring,” he said.

“But technology should support judgment, not replace it. When recruitment optimises only for speed, companies pay for it later in attrition, lost momentum and mis-hires. Meridian Talent exists to help the employers hire with clarity, not just urgency.”

Meridian Talent’s approach is built on three core principles: real specialist understanding of roles and team context, chemistry and culture fit alongside capability and a respect-driven process that treats candidates as partners.

Rather than relying solely on job descriptions, the firm spends time understanding how a business actually functions: delivery pressures, team dynamics, role expectations and future direction.

The firm focuses on specialist mid-to-senior placements, serving employers across Sri Lanka, the Asia-Pacific region and globally. Meridian Talent operates on a no- placement-no-fee basis, with clear terms designed to remove risk for employers and incentivise long-term fit.

The employers can initiate a search by filling out a detailed requirements form on the Meridian Talent website or reaching out via email. Following an onboarding call or meeting to clarify the requirements and align expectations, Meridian Talent curates a shortlist of pre-screened candidates with structured screening notes and recommendations based on comprehensive initial interviews. The employers typically receive a carefully considered shortlist within an average of seven days.

Meridian Talent is backed by Meridian Creative Solutions, a tech-enabled business consultancy that has built and scaled development operations for over seven years. This operational foundation gives Meridian Talent a practical vantage point on what ‘good’ looks like in real teams and why alignment at the point of recruitment shapes delivery, culture and trust.
