Dialog Enterprise Principal Sponsor of Sri Lanka CERT National Cyber Security Conference 2025

Dialog Enterprise, the corporate solutions arm of Dialog Axiata PLC and the leading ICT solutions provider in Sri Lanka, proudly served as the Principal Sponsor of the Sri Lanka CERT|CC Cyber Security Conference 2025, the nation’s premier platform dedicated to strengthening cybersecurity awareness and resilience across enterprises and communities. The annual conference brought together policymakers, industry leaders, and technology experts to advance the national dialogue on cyber resilience and foster collaboration towards a safer digital future.

At Dialog Enterprise, the belief that technology should empower progress while safeguarding people, businesses, and national infrastructure remains at the core of its purpose. As the trusted digital partner for organisations across Sri Lanka, Dialog Enterprise continues to enable transformation through end-to-end, secure, and future-ready ICT solutions that help enterprises innovate confidently in a rapidly evolving digital landscape. The company’s comprehensive portfolio across connectivity, cloud, cybersecurity, and managed services empowers enterprises to strengthen their digital foundations while maintaining operational integrity and trust.

Cybersecurity continues to be a vital aspect of this commitment as digital adoption accelerates across government, corporate, and SME sectors. With the growing need to protect data, operations, and customer trust, Dialog Enterprise’s continued partnership with Sri Lanka CERT|CC reflects a shared mission to enhance the nation’s cybersecurity readiness and build a culture of awareness, resilience, and preparedness against emerging threats.

Sharing his thoughts on the partnership, Mr. Thilak Pathirage, Chairman – Sri Lanka CERT, said, “We deeply value Dialog Enterprise’s continued support and collaboration in advancing the nation’s cybersecurity ecosystem. Partnerships of this nature are essential to strengthen public-private cooperation and enhance the country’s preparedness against evolving digital threats.
