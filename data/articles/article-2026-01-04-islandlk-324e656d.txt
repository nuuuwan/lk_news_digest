New model pre-school vested in Medadumbara to boost early childhood education

John Keells Foundation (JKF) and Victoria Golf Resort have formally vested the newly constructed Nithulemada Model Pre-School to the Medadumbara Pradeshiya Sabha. The facility aims to provide critical early childhood education to children aged 3-5 years in the Kandy District, addressing a two-decade-long need for a permanent space.

The project, initiated in 2020 under JKF’s ‘Praja Shakthi’ community empowerment initiative, overcame delays from the pandemic and economic challenges to complete construction in October 2025. The vesting ceremony was attended by Minister K.D. Lalkantha, provincial officials, and JKF representatives.

Speaking at the event, JKF Head of CSR Carmeline Jayasuriya highlighted the dual focus on equitable education and fostering social cohesion. “We aim to create learning environments that encourage understanding, respect, and unity among children and communities,” she said.

Minister Lalkantha emphasized the foundational importance of early childhood development. “If we truly want to change, we must begin at the very foundation,” he stated, praising the school as a potential model for future government preschools.

The event featured performances by preschool children and a demonstration by students from JKF’s English scholarship programme, showcasing the foundation’s broader educational impact in the region.
