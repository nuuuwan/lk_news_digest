EFC Solutions Unit expands as One-Stop Shop for HR needs

The Solutions Unit of the Employers’ Federation of Ceylon (EFC), which has been providing both member organizations and non-members with a broad spectrum of legal and human resource (HR) consultancy services since its launch, has significantly expanded its offerings to meet the evolving needs of Sri Lankan businesses.

Recognizing the growing demand for specialized HR solutions, the Unit now provides a range of strategic HR services designed to help organizations enhance workforce effectiveness, strengthen governance, and improve overall business performance. These services include HR audits, organizational restructuring, job evaluation and grading, compensation and benefits reviews, performance management systems, succession planning, talent management, employee engagement initiatives, and the development of HR policies and procedures.

The expanded portfolio reflects EFC’s commitment to supporting employers in navigating an increasingly complex business and regulatory environment. By combining its long-standing expertise in labour law and industrial relations with modern HR practices, the Solutions Unit offers organizations practical, customized solutions that align with their business objectives.

In addition to consultancy services, the Unit provides advisory support on workplace compliance, employee relations, disciplinary procedures, and employment contracts. Organizations can also benefit from tailored training programmes and capacity-building initiatives aimed at developing leadership and managerial capabilities.

Commenting on the expansion, EFC notes notes that people remain the most critical asset of any organization and that access to professional HR expertise is essential for sustaining competitiveness and growth. He emphasized that the Solutions Unit is positioned to serve as a trusted partner for organizations seeking high-quality, cost-effective HR and employment solutions.

Through this enhanced service offering, the EFC continues to strengthen its role as Sri Lanka’s premier employer representative body and a leading provider of workplace and HR solutions.
