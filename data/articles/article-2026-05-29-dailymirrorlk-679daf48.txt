State of Public Emergency further extended

Colombo, May 29 (Daily Mirror) - An Extraordinary Gazette Notification has been issued extending the state of public emergency in Sri Lanka, government sources said.

The notification was issued under the powers vested in President Anura Kumara Dissanayake in terms of the Public Security Ordinance, and came into effect from 28 May, according to official sources.

The extension continues the emergency legal framework currently in force across the country, enabling authorities to take measures related to public security, public order, and the maintenance of essential services.

Further details regarding the duration and specific provisions of the extension have not yet been officially released.
