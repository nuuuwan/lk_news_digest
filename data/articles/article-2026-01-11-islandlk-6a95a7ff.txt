Lending rates edge up amid private sector credit growth

Key monetary indicators for the first week of January 2026 point to sustained credit expansion to the private sector alongside a slight tightening in lending rates, as the economy continues its recovery path.

The Weekly Average Weighted Prime Lending Rate (AWPR) rose by 12 basis points to 9.19% for the week ending 09 January 2026. This increase comes despite a stable policy corridor, with the Standing Deposit Facility Rate (SDFR) and Standing Lending Facility Rate (SLFR) holding at 7.25% and 8.25%, respectively.

A standout figure is the robust growth in private sector credit. Outstanding credit to the private sector increased by Rs. 262.6 billion in November 2025 alone, recording a significant year-on-year growth of 26.0%. In contrast, Net Credit to the Government from the banking system decreased by Rs. 64.0 billion during the same month, indicating a shift in banking sector exposure.

Market liquidity remained in surplus, with total outstanding liquidity at Rs. 171.03 billion by January 9. The Average Weighted Call Money Rate (AWCMR) saw a slight decrease to 7.96%.

The positive sentiment extended to the Colombo Stock Exchange. The All Share Price Index (ASPI) gained 3.46% over the week to reach 23,654.30 points, while the S&P SL 20 Index rose 5.12% to 6,503.02 points.

Analysts see the strong private credit growth as a sign of rebounding business investment and consumption, but say the uptick in lending rates need to be monitored for its impact on borrowing costs.
