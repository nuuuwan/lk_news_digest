External uncertainties take toll on stocks

CSE activities yesterday noted some volatility due to external and internal environmental uncertainties, market analysts pointed out.

Issues such as the rupee depreciation and the duty hikes on vehicle imports along with the escalation of West Asian tensions dragged the market to red territory.

The All Share Price Index went down by 592 .28 points, while the S and P SL20 declined by 117.28 points. Turnover stood at Rs 4.88 billion with six crossings.

Those crossings were: JKH 11 million shares crossed to the tune of Rs 224 million; its shares traded at Rs 20.40, Hemas Holdings 4.9 million shares crossed to the tune of Rs 160 million; its shares traded at Rs 32.20, HNB 300,000 shares crossed for Rs 122 million; its shares sold at Rs 408, Amana Takaful 2 million shares crossed to the tune of Rs 45.5 million; its shares traded at Rs 22.20, Dialog Axiata 750,000 shares crossed for Rs 27.8 million; its shares sold at Rs 37 and Agalawatte Plantations 400,000 shares crossed to the tune of Rs 22 million; its shares traded at Rs 55.

In the retail market top companies that mainly contributed to the turnover were; ACL Cables Rs 280 million (2.8 million shares traded), Prime Lands Residencies Rs 237 million (5 million shares traded), Access Engineering Rs 149 million (1.9 million shares traded), Dialog Axiata Rs 125.8 million (780,000 shares traded), JKH Rs 125 million (6.1 million shares traded), Colombo Dockyard Rs 125 million (945,000 shares traded) and Hayleys Rs 120 million (508,000 shares traded). During the day 210 million share volumes changed hands in 48511 transactions.

It is said that manufacturing sector counters, especially JKH, performed well while the banking sector also performed well. Real estate sector, especially Prime Lands Residencies and Plantation sector counters, especially Agalwatte Plantations, performed well as well.

The telegraphic transfer rates for the American dollar were Rs326.5000 buying, Rs 333.5000 selling; the British pound was Rs 433.4209 buying, and Rs 444.8973 selling, and the euro was Rs 376.7447 buying, Rs 388.3319 selling.
