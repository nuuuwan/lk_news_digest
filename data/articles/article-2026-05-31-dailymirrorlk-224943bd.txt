Rooftop solar owners asked to disconnect systems to maintain grid balance

Colombo, May 37 (Daily Mirror) - The National System Operator (Private) Limited (NSO) has appealed to owners of rooftop solar power systems across Sri Lanka to voluntarily deactivate their installations today, from 8:00 a.m. to 3:00 p.m., in an effort to maintain the stability of the national electricity grid and prevent further power disruptions.

The request comes after a significant grid imbalance on May 30, which resulted in power outages lasting several hours in multiple parts of the country, including Colombo.

According to the NSO, the disruption was caused by an unexpected and substantial decline in electricity demand during the Vesak Poya holiday weekend, combined with unusually high daytime electricity generation from rooftop solar installations.

To address the situation on Saturday, the system operator requested solar users to voluntarily disconnect their rooftop systems until 3:00 p.m. Authorities also temporarily disconnected several 33 kV electricity feeders in regions with high concentrations of solar generation. These measures helped stabilize the network, enabling power to be fully restored to affected areas by approximately 2:15 p.m.
