Hemas Holdings’ stock trading near its estimated fair value

First Capital Research (FCR) has downgraded its recommendation for Hemas Holdings PLC (HHL) from ‘Hold’ to ‘Sell and Discontinue Coverage’, advising investors to book profits as the stock is trading near its estimated fair value.

The decision follows HHL’s broadly in-line quarterly results. The conglomerate’s Consumer Brands segment posted a healthy 21.7% year-on-year revenue growth, with strong volumes. The Healthcare segment also showed momentum, with robust performance in pharmaceutical distribution and hospital services.

Despite this solid operational performance, FCR maintains its fair value estimate for the stock at LKR 30.0 for FY26E and LKR 36.0 for FY27E. With HHL’s current share price at LKR 34.6, the implied annualised return is seen as materially below the research firm’s minimum required threshold.

The analyst report notes that the Healthcare segment, particularly manufacturing, has attractive long-term prospects while , the ongoing hospital expansion is not expected to significantly boost earnings for approximately three years.

The Consumer Brands segment is viewed as operating near peak capacity amidst competitive pressures.

FCR concludes that the stock has achieved its fair value, leading to the downgrade. The move is presented as a valuation-based call, reflecting that the period of exceptional share price appreciation may have run its course for now, rather than a critique of the company’s fundamental business strength.
