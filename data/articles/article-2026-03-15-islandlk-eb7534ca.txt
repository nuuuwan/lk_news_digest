Agrovista Plantation plants 250,000 trees under ‘One Million Trees’ drive

Agrovista Plantation has announced significant early progress in its “One Million Trees for the Motherland by 2030” initiative, surpassing 250,000 trees planted since the program launched in 2024.

The ambitious environmental drive aims to plant one million trees across Sri Lanka by 2030 as part of the company’s corporate social responsibility commitment. It focuses heavily on engaging school children, providing resources and guidance for students to plant, nurture, and protect trees.

By involving young people in conservation activities, Agrovista seeks to instill lifelong environmental awareness and responsibility. The broader vision encompasses climate resilience, biodiversity conservation, and expanding green landscapes nationwide.

The company invites individuals, organizations, and schools to support the mission through participation, plant donations, or partnerships.

“Through collective effort, we can build a greener Sri Lanka for future generations,” a company spokesperson said.
