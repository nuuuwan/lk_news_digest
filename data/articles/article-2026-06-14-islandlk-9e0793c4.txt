Sea Master opens new logistics centre in Wattala at a cost exceeding Rs. 500 million

On June 11, Sea Master Group inaugurated its second Integrated Logistics Centre (ILC) in Hendala, Wattala, just 8–9 kilometers from the Port of Colombo. The total investment in the project exceeds Rs. 500 million.

The 5.5-acre facility aims to strengthen Sri Lanka’s logistics infrastructure and support the nation’s goal of becoming a regional logistics hub. Chairman Taarik Booso called the opening a milestone in the company’s 23-year history, emphasising its commitment to innovation, service excellence, and local economic growth.

The first phase is already operational, offering container storage, repair and maintenance, and laden container handling. A weighbridge and 50 reefer plugging points will be fully operational within a month. A second phase, set to begin in six months, will add three acres with modern warehousing and expanded container storage.

Managing Director Anuruddha Withanarachchi noted that the centre’s proximity to the Port of Colombo will reduce turnaround times and logistics costs for customers. CEO Layan de Silva added that the facility will create jobs and support end-to-end supply chain solutions.

Booso highlighted the company’s philosophy: “Handled Locally, Recognised Globally,” combining local expertise with international standards. The project is expected to boost cargo efficiency, support trade, and advance Sri Lanka’s ambition to become a South Asian logistics and maritime hub.
