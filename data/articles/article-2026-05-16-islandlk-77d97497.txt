Sampath Bank launches SME business card

Sampath Bank has introduced the Mastercard Business Credit Card, a new financial solution aimed at supporting the operational and cash flow requirements of small and medium enterprises (SMEs) in Sri Lanka.

Developed in partnership with Mastercard, the card is designed to provide SMEs with greater financial flexibility, improved expense management and enhanced access to digital payment solutions.

The bank said the card offers flexible repayment facilities, including instalment plans, customisable credit limits based on business performance, and loyalty rewards on business-related spending. Cardholders will also receive access to local and international promotional offers, enhanced security features and online tools to monitor and settle payments.

Commenting on the launch, Sampath Bank Chief Business Intelligence Officer Darshin Pathinayake said the initiative reflects the bank’s commitment to empowering SMEs with practical financial solutions to support growth and strengthen operational control.

The bank has also launched the Mastercard Purchasing Card for corporates to streamline procurement and expense management. The solution includes transaction controls, merchant category restrictions, automated bill payments and consolidated reporting features.

Mastercard Sri Lanka and Maldives Country Manager Sandun Hapugoda said the partnership highlights Mastercard’s continued commitment to advancing digital payment solutions for businesses in Sri Lanka.

Sampath Bank said the dual launch forms part of its broader strategy to deliver innovative financial solutions to SMEs and corporates amid the country’s growing digital economy.
