IMF approves USD 695 m tranche as Sri Lanka programme stays on track

The International Monetary Fund (IMF) Executive Board has approved the combined fifth and sixth reviews of Sri Lanka’s Extended Fund Facility (EFF) arrangement, paving the way for the immediate disbursement of approximately USD 695 million, in a move widely seen as a strong endorsement of the country’s ongoing economic recovery and reform agenda.

The latest approval brings total IMF financial support disbursed to Sri Lanka under the EFF programme to several billion dollars since the facility was launched in 2023 to stabilise the crisis-hit economy.

Deputy Governor of the Central Bank of Sri Lanka Dr. Chandranath Amarasekara described the IMF Executive Board decision as a major milestone that demonstrates continued international confidence in Sri Lanka’s macroeconomic reform programme.

“This is another important step in Sri Lanka’s economic stabilisation process. The successful completion of the reviews reflects the authorities’ commitment to maintaining prudent macroeconomic policies, restoring fiscal discipline, strengthening governance and ensuring debt sustainability,” Dr. Amarasekara said.

He noted that the IMF-backed reforms had already contributed significantly towards restoring macroeconomic stability, rebuilding investor confidence and improving the country’s external sector performance.

Economic analysts say the approval is expected to further strengthen market confidence at a time when Sri Lanka is gradually emerging from its worst economic crisis in decades, characterised by sovereign default, foreign exchange shortages and soaring inflation.

Officials pointed out that the country has recorded notable improvements in key economic indicators, including easing inflation, stabilisation of the exchange rate, improved foreign reserve levels and renewed confidence in the banking and financial sectors.

The IMF Board’s endorsement also signals that Sri Lanka’s reform programme remains broadly on track despite global economic uncertainties and domestic fiscal challenges.

Dr. Amarasekera expressed appreciation to IMF officials Evan Papageorgiou, Martha Tesfaye Woldemichael and the IMF staff for their continued engagement and close collaboration with Sri Lanka during the review process.

Special commendation was also extended to Dr. Sujeetha Jegajeevan, Sri Lanka’s Alternate Executive Director at the IMF, for representing the country at the Executive Board discussions.

Economists believe the latest tranche would provide further support for Sri Lanka’s balance of payments position while helping sustain critical reforms aimed at achieving long-term economic resilience and inclusive growth.

The IMF’s latest decision is also likely to boost investor sentiment and support ongoing negotiations linked to debt restructuring and international financing cooperation.
