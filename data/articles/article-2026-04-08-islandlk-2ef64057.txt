IMF announcement on tranches takes CSE from gloom to light

Although the CSE was enveloped in some negativity at the beginning of trading yesterday, later some positive trends were noted when the government announced that IMF had decided to release the 5th and 6th tranches of its package of assistance amounting to US$ 700 million soon.

At the end of the session the All Share Price Index was up by 72.89 points, while the S and P SL20 went down by 18.90 points. Turnover stood at Rs 1.88 billion with six crossings.

In the retail market top seven companies that mainly contributed to the turnover were; NDB Rs 455 million (4.1 million shares traded), Tokyo Cement (Non-Voting) Rs 72.5 million (one million shares traded), Commercial Bank Rs 51 million (253,000 shares traded), Acme Printers Rs 49.7 million (7.9 million shares traded), JKH Rs 49.1 million (2.6 million shares traded), HNB (Non-Voting) Rs 45 million (226,000 shares traded) and Chevron Lubricant Rs 40.7 million (226,000 shares traded).

During the day 48.7 million share volumes changed hands in 19156 transactions.

It is said that banking sector counters, especially HNB and NDB, led the market while the manufacturing sector, especially JKH, and construction sector counters, especially Tokyo Cement, performed well.

Vidullanka announced that it has authorized the acquisition of a 100 percent equity stake in Bhoruka Power Lanka, which owns and operates the 4.5MW Gurugoda mini hydro power plant situated in Aranayake.

Siyapatha Finance will list Rs 3.75 billion of 11.50 percent debentures on April 7, CSE sources said.

Yesterday, the rupee was quoted at Rs 315.42/50 to the US dollar in the spot market, from Rs 315.40/50 the previous day, dealers said, while bond yields were broadly steady.

A bond maturing on 15.03.2028 was quoted at 9.45/50 percent, down from 9.45/60 percent.

A bond maturing on 15.12.2029 was quoted at 9.85/95 percent, down from 9.90/95 percent.

A bond maturing on 01.06.2033 was quoted at 11.00/10 percent, up from 10.95/11.05 percent.
