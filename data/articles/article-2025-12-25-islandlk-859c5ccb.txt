Significant institutional participation props up CSE

Significant institutional participation buoyed trading activity on the Colombo Stock Exchange (CSE) yesterday, marking an improvement over the previous session, according to market analysts.

Amid those developments both indices moved upwards.All Share Price Index up by 32.05 points while S and P SL20 up by 15.33 points. Turnover stood at Rs 4.1 billion with eight crossings.

Those crossings were reported in JKH which crossed 66 million shares to the tune of Rs 1.4 billion and its share price traded at Rs 21.10, Amana Tackful 4.3 million shares crossed to the tune of Rs 95 million and its share price traded at Rs 22, Digital Mobility Solutions 500,000 shares crossed to the tune of Rs 72.5 million and its share price traded at Rs 145, People’s Leasing three million shares crossed to the tune of Rs 72 million and its share price traded at Rs 24, Laughs Gas 650,000 shares crossed to the tune of Rs 44.2 million and its share price traded at Rs 58, Royal Ceramics one million shares crossed to the tune of Rs 44 million and its share price traded at Rs 44, Sampath Bank 230,000 shares crossed to the tune of Rs 32.7 million and its share price traded at Rs 142 and Tokyo Cement (Non-Voting) 250,000 shares crossed to the tune of Rs 23.1 million and its share price traded at Rs 92.50.

In the retail market top seven companies that have mainly contributed to the turnover were  Colombo Dockyard ( Right Issue) Rs 316 million (5.6 million shares traded), JKH Rs 283 million (13.5 million shares traded), Colombo Dockyards Rs 265 million (two million shares traded), Prime Lands Residencies Rs 79 million (2.5 million shares traded), Tokyo Cement Rs 60 million (532,000 shares traded), DFCC Rs 51 million (357,000 shares traded) and Sierra Cables Rs 51 million (1.6 million shares traded). During the day 147.2 million shares volumes changed hands in 22781 transactions.
