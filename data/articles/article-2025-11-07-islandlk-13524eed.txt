Bourse upbeat over positive effects of Budget 2026; investor sentiment up

The CSE was upbeat over the 2026 budget yesterday, with the consequence that both indices moved upwards amid positive investor sentiments.The All Share Price Index went up by 225.7 points, while the S and P SL20 rose by 44.51 points. Turnover stood at Rs 6.7 billion with 11 crossings.

In the retail market top seven companies that mainly contributed to the turnover were; Laugfs Gas Rs 446 million (6.2 million shares traded), Sampath Bank Rs 301 million (two million shares traded), Bogala Graphite Rs 290 million (2.2 million shares traded), Sunshine Holdings Rs 242 million (6.2 million shares traded), JKH Rs 240 million (10.9 million shares traded), Colombo Dockyard Rs 219 million (480000 shares traded) and Kotagala Plantations Rs 205 million (18 million shares traded). During the day 209 million share volumes changed hands in 44670 transactions.

It is said that mixed reactions were noted in most of sectors. The banking and finance sector performed well; especially DFCC, while the mining and plantations sectors too performed well; especially, Bogala Graphite and Kotagala Plantations.

Furher, Resus Energy said it will seek shareholder approval for a share sub-division which will see an ordinary share split into 5 ordinary shares.

The subdivision will see 86,184,827 ordinary shares increased to 430,924,135, CSE sources said.

The subdivision of shares will not result in any change to the stated capital of the company, which will remain at Rs 967,483,260, Resus Energy said.

Yesterday the rupee opened weaker at Rs 304.90/305.05 to the US dollar in the spot market from Rs 304.85/95 the previous day, dealers said, while bond yields recovered from the previous close.
