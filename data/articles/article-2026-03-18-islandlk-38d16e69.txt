Bourse loses 2000 ASPI points over five consecutive days

The CSE lost 2000 All Share Price Index points over the last five consecutive days, as Middle East-linked uncertainties lingered.

This situation triggered an oversold situation of valuable stocks at low prices but the scenario changed yesterday due to President Anura Kumara Dissanayake’s statement that the government is to place emergency purchase arrangements for gas and fuel to address the current crisis situation.

Amid those developments both indices moved upwards. The All Share Price Index went up by 335 points while S and P SL20 rose by 42.73 points.

Turnover stood at Rs 5.27 billion with seven crossings. Those crossings were reported in HNB, where 1.5 million shares crossed to the tune of Rs 608 million; its shares traded at Rs 395, JKH 18.9 million shares crossed for Rs 347 million; its shares traded at Rs 18.40, Pickme 300,000 shares crossed to the tune of Rs 63 million; its shares sold at Rs 134.

Commercial Bank 300,000 shares crossed for Rs 38 million; its shares traded at Rs 193, NTB 200,000 shares crossed for Rs 56.4 million; its shares traded at Rs 282, Nawaloka 2.8 million shares crossed for Rs 28.4 million; its shares sold at Rs 10.10 and Hemas Holdings one million shares crossed to the tune of Rs 28 million; its shares fetched Rs 28.

In the retail market top seven companies that mainly contributed to the turnover were; HNB Rs 463 million (1.1 million shares traded), Colombo Dockyard Rs 309 million (2.7 million shares traded), Commercial Bank Rs 273 million (1.4 million shares traded), JKH Rs 253 million (13.6 million shares traded), Prime Lands Residencies Rs 167 million (3.7 million shares traded), Sunshine Holdings Rs 161 million (6.1 million shares traded) and Softlogic Capital Rs 147 million (11.2 million shares traded). During the day 216 million share volumes changed hands in 39012 transactions.

It is said that banking sector counters, especially Commercial Bank and HNB, performed well while the manufacturing sector, especially JKH, performed well. Real estate sector counters, especially Prime Lands Residencies, also traded well.

Ambeon Holdings has announced a share sub-division ,which will see a share split into four.

The subdivision will see 356,869,666 ordinary voting shares increased to 1,427,478,664, CSE sources said.

The subdivision of shares will not result in any change to the stated capital of the company, which will remain at Rs 5,331,775,177 the company said.

The sub-division is subject to Colombo Stock Exchange (CSE) and shareholder approval.

Yesterday, the rupee was quoted at Rs 311.25/35 to the US dollar in the spot market, improving from Rs 311.30/40 the previous day, dealers said, while bond yields were broadly steady.

An auction of Rs 120,000 million Treasury bills was ongoing.
