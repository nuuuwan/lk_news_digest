DFCC Bank-Standard Chartered Bank Sri Lanka branch deal to be completed by July 31

DFCC Bank will complete the acquisition of the wealth and retail banking business of Standard Chartered Bank Sri Lanka branch by July 31, CSE sources said.

“The proposed acquisition is scheduled to be concluded by 31 July 2026, the lender said.

A further announcement will be made upon completion of the transaction.

In November last year, DFCC Bank announced it had reached a deal to buy the retail and wealth management business of Standard Chartered Bank in Sri Lanka for Rs 3.7 billion.

The transaction was supposed to be concluded in early 2026, the bank said, using internal funds.

However, the transaction slowed down due to a number of external and internal factors.

Amid those developments both indices moved upwards. The All Share Price Index went up by 50.02 points, while the S and P SL20 rose by 3.15 points. Turnover stood at Rs 3.85 billion with seven crossings.

Those crossings were; CIC Holdings 37 million shares crossed to the tune of Rs 1.18 billion; its shares traded at Rs 32, Singer (SriLanka) 1.8 million shares crossed for Rs 139 million; its shares traded at Rs 75, Commercial Bank 500,000 shares crossed for Rs 105 million; its shares traded at Rs 210, JKH 4.4 million shares crossed for Rs 90 million; its shares traded at Rs 20.40, Digital Mobility Solutions 300,000 shares crossed for Rs 50.4 million; its shares sold at Rs 168.

Aitken Spence 200,000 shares crossed to the tune of Rs 28.6 million; its shares sold at Rs 143. During the day 152 million share volumes changed hands in 22875 transactions.

It’s said that CIC and Singer (Sri Lanka) performed well. Banking sector counters, especially Commercial Bank, performed well, while the manufacturing sector, especially JKH, also did well at the floor.

Yesterday, the rupee was quoted at Rs 333.00/334.00 to the US dollar in the spot market, from Rs 333.00/334.50 the previous day.

Meanwhile in the secondary market, bond yields extended the rally and rates dropped further, dealers said.

The telegraphic transfer rate for Sri Lanka’s rupee against the US dollar was 329.00 buying, 338.00 selling; the euro was 378.7135 selling, 392.6305 buying; and the pound was 439.9314 buying, 453.9770 selling.

An auction of Rs 701 billion s treasury bills was scheduled for today.
