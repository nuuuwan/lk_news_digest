The Book Studio revolutionises reading with first automated book vending machine

From left: Digital Marketing Executives Hanah Rafeeq and Melani Pathiratne, Sector Head – Finance Pathum Sanjeewa, Deputy General Manager Nalinda Fernando, Assistant General Manager Lasitha Ekanayake, and CEO/Director Kushan Gunasekera

The Book Studio Ltd., has introduced Sri Lanka’s first fully automated book vending machine, marking a significant innovation in the country’s publishing and retail landscape.

Redefining how readers can discover and purchase books with instant self-service, this landmark initiative represents a strategic synergy of technology and convenience, designed to make literature more accessible to the community.

Aligned with the core belief that “everywhere can be a reading space,” this initiative supports holistic well-being by integrating a culture of learning and curiosity directly into everyday high-traffic environments. The automated unit allows readers to walk away with their selected read in an instant, bridging the gap between busy modern lifestyles and the simple pleasures of reading.

CEO/Director Kushan Gunasekera said: “It gives us great pleasure to introduce this innovation to Sri Lanka via The Book Studio. This is a milestone in our journey and a testament to our vision for a more well-read Sri Lanka. By leveraging automated technology, we are making the act of discovering a new book as seamless and effortless as possible. Through this automated book vending machine, we have unveiled a new channel for schools, universities, banks, corporates, hospitals, and high-footfall locations to provide books, stationery, and learning essentials with convenience, visibility, and accessibility.”

The Book Studio is committed to expanding the initiative islandwide. This launch marks the first step in a broader journey toward making reading fun, innovative, and effortless for everyone.

As part of its long-term growth and innovation strategy, The Book Studio will additionally be unveiling two new flagship outlets in the southern and northern regions of Sri Lanka. Conceptualised as next-generation literary and lifestyle spaces, these outlets will move beyond the conventional bookstore format to offer a refined and immersive customer experience. Through a seamless integration of contemporary retail design, carefully curated collections, interactive environments, and technology-enabled convenience, the new outlets aim to set a new benchmark for book retail in Sri Lanka and transform the way readers engage with books and learning spaces.
