It’s all over for Maxi Rozairo

The legendary Sri Lankan singer and musician, Maxi Rozairo, widely recognised for his decades-long career as a leading vocalist in the music scene, is no more.

He has been ailing for quite a while and passed away last Saturday, 27th, December, according to his brother, Noel Rozairo, who is based in Australia.

Noel was here, in town, just recently, to check on his brother’s health.

Maxi, I’m told, began his professional career in 1966, with the band Soundwaves, and later joined the group Fireflies and then the Spitfires in 1970.

He has also performed alongside other Sri Lankan stars, like Noeline Honter and Mignonne Fernando, and has been a staple at high-profile venues, such as the California Grill.

Recalling a performance she did with Maxi at The Island Music Awards, 2014, Noeline said the crowd stood up and applauded when they did ‘Beauty And The Beast.’

In 2010, Maxi shifted his focus toward mentoring the next generation of performers and stablished his own school of music to teach and guide singers on the finer points of vocal performance, presentation skills and stagecraft.

Maxi served as the President of the Burgher Association in Sri Lanka, where he worked to organise and represent the interests of the Burgher community.
