Some bounce in bourse inspired by positive uptick in economy

The CSE witnessed some positive investor sentiment yesterday mainly due to the economy moving in a positive direction against the backdrop of the overall budget deficit declining to 37 percent of GDP.

The market was mainly driven by Colombo Dockyards. Both indices showed an upward trend. The All Share Price Index went up 53.49 points, while the S and P SL20 rose up by 10.19 points. Turnover stood at Rs 3.71 billion with 11 crossings.

The top seven crossings were reported in DFCC, where 1.4 million shares crossed to the tune of Rs 200 million; its shares traded at Rs 144.50, JKH 7.3 million shares crossed for Rs 153.4 million; its shares traded at Rs 3.90, Commercial Bank 500,000 shares crossed for Rs 99.5 million; its shares sold at Rs 199, Richard Pieris 1.4 million shares crossed to the tune of Rs 59 million; its shares fetched Rs 41, People’s Leasing and Finance 2 million shares crossed for Rs 49.5 million; its shares sold at Rs 24, Cargills Bank five million shares crossed to the tune of Rs 48.5 million; its shares traded at Rs 9.70 and Lankem Ceylon 964,000 shares crossed for Rs 39.5 million; its shares traded at Rs 41.

In the retail market top seven companies that mainly contributed to the turnover were; Colombo Dockyard (Right) Rs 862 million (13 million shares traded), Colombo Dockyards (Voting) Rs 585 million (3.6 million shares traded) , JKH Rs 86 million (four million shares traded), Tokyo Cement (Non-Voting) Rs 72 million (780,000 shares traded), HNB ( Non-Voting) Rs 59 million (188,000 shares traded), DFCC Rs 51 million (353,000 shares traded) and ACL Cables Rs 43 million (501,000 shares traded). During the day 108.7 million share volumes changed hands in 29511 transactions.

It is said that banking sector counters, especially DFCC Bank, performed well, while the manufacturing sector, especially JKH, performed well too.

Yesterday the rupee was quoted at Rs 309.60/75 to the US dollar in the spot market, broadly unchanged from 309.65/75 last Friday, having depreciated in recent weeks, dealers said, while bond yields were lower on the short end. Accordingly:

A bond maturing on 15.12.2029 was quoted at 9.68/75 percent.

A bond maturing on 15.03.2031 was quoted at 9.95/10.00 percent.

A bond maturing on 01.10.2032 was quoted at 10.30/35 percent, from 10.32/35 percent.

A bond maturing on 01.11.2033 was quoted at 10.50/55 percent, from 10.45/60 percent.

An issue of Rs 55,000 million Treasury Bonds is scheduled for December 30 and an auction of Rs 120,000 million Treasury bills is due on December 31.
