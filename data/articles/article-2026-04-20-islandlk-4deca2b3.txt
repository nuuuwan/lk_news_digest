Middle East war continues to wreck stock investor hopes

The CSE was trending down yesterday as investors continued to be unsettled by the war situation in West Asia, brokers said.

‘The market is still very proportional to the Middle Eastern conflict, therefore both indices showed a downward trend, they explained. The All Share Price Index went down by 203.6 points, while the S and P SL20 declined by 39.43 points. Turnover stood at Rs 4.11 billion with four crossings.

Those crossings were; Access Engineering 900,000 shares crossed for Rs 69.3 million and its shares traded at Rs 77, Janashakthi Insurance 1 million shares crossed to the tune of Rs 50 million and its shares traded at Rs 50, Central Finance 100,000 shares crossed for Rs 25 million; its shares traded at Rs 250 and Haycarb 198,000 shares crossed for Rs 24.6 million; its shares sold at Rs 124.

In the retail market top seven companies that mainly contributed to the turnover were; Softlogic Finance Rs 328 million (36.9 million shares traded), Hikkaduwa Beach Resort Rs 263 million (40.6 million shares traded), Softlogic Capital Rs 226 million (12.9 million shares traded), Softlogic Holdings Rs 217 million (12 million shares traded), JKH Rs 137 million (6.7 million shares traded) , Asiri Hospital Rs 124 million (three million shares traded) and Housing and Developments Finance Rs 123 million (1.8 million shares traded). During the day 257.8 million shares changed hands in 43845 transactions. Insurance and financial sector performed well, while the construction related companies, especially Access Engineering, played an important role at the floor.

Yesterday the rupee was quoted at Rs 316.40/80 to the US dollar in the spot market, weaker from Rs 316.50/70 the previous close, dealers said, while bond yields were steady.
