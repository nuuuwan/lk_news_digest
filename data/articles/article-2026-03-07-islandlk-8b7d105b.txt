SLT Group reports strong FY 2025 performance driven by cost savings and efficiency

The SLT Group recorded strong financial growth in the year ended December 31, 2025, driven by cost optimisation, reduced finance costs and steady expansion across its fixed and mobile segments.

The Group reported a 221% surge in Profit After Tax to Rs. 10 billion, up from Rs. 3.1 billion in 2024, supported by disciplined expense management and improving operational efficiency. Group revenue rose 3% to Rs. 114.2 billion, while operating profit grew 26.9% to Rs. 14.2 billion. Interest costs declined by 21% to Rs. 7.05 billion as debt levels were reduced.

The mobile subsidiary Mobitel (Sri Lanka) delivered exceptional performance, posting a PAT of Rs. 3.1 billion — a 2,123% increase year-on-year — supported by stronger data revenue, value-added services and enterprise solutions. Revenue grew 5% to Rs. 47.9 billion amid rising data consumption and subscriber growth.

The parent company Sri Lanka Telecom PLC also maintained steady growth, with broadband expansion, particularly fibre-to-the-home (FTTH) services, driving revenue. Operating profit rose 27% to Rs. 9.3 billion, supported by lower financing costs.

Chairman Dr. Mothilal de Silva said the performance reflected the Group’s strategic focus on cost control and sustainable growth. He highlighted the company’s role in restoring network infrastructure following Cyclone Ditwah, where more than 98% of damaged sites were recovered within a week.

Chief Executive Officer Riyaaz Rasheed said the Group was entering 2026 with renewed focus on innovation, customer engagement and network expansion. He noted that the acquisition of 5G spectrum and islandwide deployment would support Sri Lanka’s digital transformation.

The Group said future plans would prioritise fibre expansion, subscriber growth and enterprise digital services.
