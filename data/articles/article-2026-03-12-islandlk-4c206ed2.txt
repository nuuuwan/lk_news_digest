Bourse ambles from bearishness to sprite despite oil price surge

Stock trading was somewhat bearish at the outset yesterday due to the surge in oil prices in the global market but later recovered when oil prices settled at US$100 per barrel.Amid those developments both indices moved downwards. The All Share Price Index went down by 333.95 points, while the S and P SL20 declined by 92.04 points.

Turnover stood at Rs 4.1 billion with ten crossings. Those crossings were reported in Melstacorp where 2.68 million shares crossed to the tune of Rs 442 million; its shares traded at Rs 155, ACL Cables 2.5 million shares crossed for Rs 234 million and its shares traded at Rs 94, Chevron Lubricant 765,900 shares crossed for Rs 141.7 million and its shares sold at Rs 185.

Tokyo Cement 1 1 million shares crossed to the tune of Rs 110 million; its shares traded at Rs 99, Access Engineering 1.46 million shares crossed for Rs 107.5 million; its shares sold at Rs 73, Colombo Dockyard 445,000 shares crossed for Rs 57.8 million; its shares sold at Rs 130 and Hemas Holdings 1.6 million shares crossed for Rs 40.5 million; its shares sold at Rs 30.50.

In the retail market top seven companies that mainly contributed to the turnover were; Lanka Credit and Business Finance Rs 291 million (30.5 million shares traded),Access Engineering Rs 202 million (2.7 million shares traded), JKH Rs 157 million (7.8 million shares traded), ACL Cables Rs 140 million (1.5 million shares traded), Sampath Bank Rs 135 million (877,000 shares traded), Softlogic Capital Rs 121 million (9.6 million shares traded) and LOLC Holdings Rs 97.60 million (177,000 shares traded). During the day 139.7 million shares volumes changed hands in 30446 transactions.

It is said that the capital goods sector performed well, while banking, manufacturing and construction sector counters performed well too.

DFCC Bank said it will list its Blue Bond on the Luxembourg Stock Exchange. The bond is already listed on the Colombo Stock Exchange. The stock was down 2.44 percent at Rs.139.75 during intra-day trading.

.Yesterday, the rupee closed at 310.85/95 to the US dollar in the spot market Wednesday, stronger than 310.95/311.05 the previous day, dealers said, while bond yields edged up. A bond maturing on15.12.2026 closed at 8.10/20 percent.
