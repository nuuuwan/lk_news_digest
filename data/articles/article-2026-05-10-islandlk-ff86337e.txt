IIHS and Akutagawa HRM launch pathway for Sri Lankan youth to build healthcare careers in Japan

The International Institute of Health Sciences (IIHS), in partnership with Akutagawa HRM Company, has launched the Japanese Care Professional Programme to help Sri Lankan youth build careers in Japan’s healthcare sector. With an ageing population and a shortage of over 430,000 caregivers, Japan offers significant long-term opportunities for skilled international professionals.

Unlike traditional migration destinations such as the UK, Australia, and Canada—where visa processes are becoming more restrictive—this programme utilises Japan’s Specified Skilled Worker (SSW) visa framework, which prioritises practical skills, language proficiency, and industry readiness. The four-year pathway integrates professional healthcare education with Japanese language training and cultural preparation, offering routes to nursing roles and higher-skilled positions.

Starting salaries are estimated at around LKR 450,000 per month, with annual earnings potentially exceeding LKR 5.4 million within five years. Participants may recover initial costs within months of employment. Financing options are available through institutions such as the National Savings Bank (NSB). Additional support includes accommodation, travel arrangements, and long-term visa pathways leading to permanent residency and family migration.

IIHS CEO Dr. Kithsiri Edirisinghe described the initiative as a shift toward skills-based global mobility, while Akutagawa HRM President Yuma Akutagawa highlighted the value of international partnerships in addressing Japan’s workforce needs.
