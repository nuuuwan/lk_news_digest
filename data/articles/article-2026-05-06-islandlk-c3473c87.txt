Positive news from war front energizes stock market

CSE trading indicated an improved investor sentiment yesterday due to positive information coming in on the war situation in West Asian.

Both indices moved upwards. The All Share Price Index went up by 155 points, while the S and P SL20 rose by 39 points. Turnover stood at Rs 4 billion with five crossings.

Those crossings were: Hatton Plantations 10 million shares crossed to the tune of Rs 162 million and its shares traded at Rs 25.60, Commercial Bank 500,000 shares crossed for Rs 105 million; its shares traded at Rs 210, HNB (Non-Voting) 260,000 shares crossed for Rs 87 million; its shares traded at Rs 350, Amana Takaful 3.5 million shares crossed for Rs 77 million; its shares sold at Rs 22 and CIC Holdings 1 million shares crossed for Rs 26 million; its shares traded at Rs 26.

In the retail market top seven companies that mainly contributed to the turnover were; Citrus Leisure Rs 415 million (83 million shares traded), Janashakthi Rs 370 million (26 million shares traded), Lanka IOC Rs 185 million (1.3 million shares traded), Asiri Hospital Rs 114 million (2.5 million shares traded) , Softlogic Capital Rs 106 million (6.4 million shares traded) and Lanka Realty Investments Rs 96 million (1.8 million shares traded). During the day 377 million share volumes changed hands in 33000 transactions.

It is said that the leisure sector, especially Citrus Leisure, performed well, while plantation sector counters, especially Hatton Plantations, also performed well. Banking sector counters also performed satisfactorily.

Yesterday the rupee was quoted at Rs 320.10/60 to the US dollar in the spot market on Wednesday, weaker from Rs 319.90/320.40 the previous day, dealers said, while bond yields were steady.

An auction of 100,000 million rupees Treasury bills was ongoing.

A bond maturing on 01.07.2028 was quoted at 9.75/80 percent, down from 9.75/85 percent.

A bond maturing on 15.12.2029 was quoted at 10.10/20 percent, up from 10.05/15 percent.

A bond maturing on 01.07.2030 was quoted flat at 10.20/25 percent.

A bond maturing on 01.06.2033 was quoted at 11.00/15 percent, up from 11.00/10 percent.

A bond maturing on 15.06.2034 was quoted flat at 11.25/30 percent.
