ComBank targets Sri Lanka’s global freelancers with ‘ComBank GIG+’ to formalise their forex earnings

Commercial Bank of Ceylon has launched ‘ComBank GIG+,’ a new banking solution aimed at Sri Lanka’s growing digital workforce, including freelancers, digital entrepreneurs, and social media influencers.

The account allows individuals and businesses earning foreign currency—including via PayPal-linked withdrawals—to formalise their income streams.

Offered in LKR and major foreign currencies (USD, EUR, GBP, AUD), it is available to Sri Lankan citizens aged 18+ and registered businesses.

The account provides access to savings, credit, and investments based on verifiable foreign earnings.

Benefits include first-year fee waivers on credit cards, preferential exchange rates, and seamless PayPal integration via ComBank Digital. The launch supports financial inclusion and economic contribution from the gig economy.
