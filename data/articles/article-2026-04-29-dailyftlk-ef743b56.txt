Govt. explores faster, digital overhaul of Aswesuma beneficiary identification

A high-level discussion was held at the Finance Ministry yesterday to streamline and strengthen the identification and listing of beneficiaries under the Aswesuma Welfare Program.

The meeting focused on revising existing eligibility criteria, expediting the identification process, and leveraging digitalisation to improve accuracy and efficiency.

Officials also examined both long-term and immediate measures, including potential amendments to the relevant Act alongside practical steps that could be implemented in the short term.

Key administrative aspects were also discussed, such as filling existing vacancies, increasing the involvement of Grama Niladharis in the assessment process, and refining payment mechanisms.

The move is expected to enhance the transparency, speed, and accuracy of beneficiary selection under the Aswesuma program, ensuring support reaches eligible households more effectively.

The discussion was attended by Rural Development, Social Security and Community Empowerment Minister Upali Pannalage, Labour Minister and Finance and Planning Minister Dr. Anil Jayantha Fernando, Digital Economy Deputy Minister Eranga Weeraratne, along with officials of the Welfare Benefits Board.
