Colombo bourse wakes up late, ends day on a busier note

The Colombo Stock Exchange(CSE) witnessed a tale of two sessions on Friday, transforming from a quiet, listless arena in the morning to a noticeably active one by the afternoon, signalling a shift in investor sentiment as the trading day unfolded.

Amid those developments both indices moved downward. All Share Price Index down by 214.10 points while S and P SL20 down by 87.90. Turnover stood at Rs 3.97 billion with nine crossings.

Top seven companies that have mainly contributed to the turnover were JKH six million shares crossed to the tune of Rs 125.6 million and its share price traded at Rs 20.90, HNB 250,000 shares crossed to the tune of Rs 96 million and its share price traded at Rs 384, Hemas Holdings 1.7 million shares crossed to the tune of Rs 56.3 million and its share price traded at 34, VallibelOne 471,000 shares crossed to the tune of Rs 44.3 million and its share price traded at Rs 94, Cargills 30000 shares crossed to the tune of Rs 24 million and its share price traded at Rs 800, RIL Properties 700,000 shares crossed to the tune of Rs 21.4 million and its share price traded at Rs 30.50 and Sanasa Developments Bank 350,000 shares crossed to the tune of Rs 21 million and its share price traded at Rs 50.

In the retail market top seven companies that have mainly contributed to the turnover were Commercial Bank Rs 243 million (1.2 million shares traded), HNB Rs 175 million (551,000 shares traded), Sampath Bank Rs 151 million (one million shares traded), Colombo Dockyards Rs 127.7 million (one million shares traded), Hemas Holdings Rs 126.8 million (3.7 million shares traded), Access Engineering Rs 122 million (1.7 million shares traded) and JKH Rs 118 million (5.6 million shares traded). During the day 134 million shares volumes changed hands in 44000 transactions.

It is said that banking sector counters were well performed especially HNB and Commercial Bank. Further realestate sector counters especially RIL Properties also performed well.
