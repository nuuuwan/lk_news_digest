Bourse notches 12 crossings but trading levels leave much to be desired

CSE trading did not reach satisfactory levels yesterday due to month- end profit- takings.The All Share Price Index went up by 8.16 points, while the S and P SL20 rose by 6.78 points. Turnover stood at Rs 6.2 billion with 12 crossings.

Top seven crossings were; Laugfs Gas 3.6 million shares crossed to the tune of Rs 244 million; its shares traded at Rs 62, Colombo Dockyards 650,000 shares crossed for Rs 134 million; its shares traded at Rs 210, Seylan Bank one million shares crossed for Rs 110 million; its shares traded at Rs 110, JKH 4.5 million shares crossed for Rs 95.9 million; its shares sold at Rs 21.40, Seylan Bank (Non-Voting) 1.1 million shares crossed to the tune of Rs 90.6 million; its shares traded at Rs 80, Richard Pieris two million shares crossed for Rs 67.5 million; its shares sold at Rs 34.60 and Lanka IOC 480,000 shares crossed to the tune of Rs 53.30 million; its shares fetched Rs 132.

In the retail market companies that mainly contributed to the turnover were; LOLC Finance Rs 367 million (53 million shares traded), Royal Ceramics Rs 260 million (5.6 million shares traded), Brown’s Investments Rs 172 million (2.2 million shares traded), Sierra Cables Rs 161 million (5.3 million shares traded), Serendib Lands Rs 153 million (102,000 shares traded), Colombo Dockyards Rs 150 million (728,000 shares traded) and Dipped Products Rs 144 million (2.1 million shares traded). During the day 226 million share volumes changed hands in 40000 transactions.

It is said that manufacturing and banking and financial sectors performed well.

Yesterday, the rupee was quoted at Rs 303.50/70 to the US dollar, slightly recovering from Rs 303.65/75 the previous day, while secondary market bond yields dropped on the 2028-2029 tenors, dealers said.

A bond maturing on 01.10.2032 was quoted at 10.55/65 percent.

The telegraphic transfer rates for the American dollar was 300.2000 buying, 307.2000 selling; the British pound was 398.9952 buying, and 410.3570 selling, and the euro was 346.3289 buying, 357.6921 selling.
