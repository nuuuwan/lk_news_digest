A bearish sluggishness overcomes share market; indices down

Amid bearish sluggishness both CSE indices moved downwards yesterday. The All Share Price Index went down by 182.14 points, while the S and P SL20 declined by 32.17 points.

Turnover stood at Rs 2.34 billion with seven crossings. Those crossings were reported in Hemas Holdings where four million shares crossed to the tune of Rs 143 million; its shares traded at Rs 35.50, DIMO 55000 shares crossed for Rs 136.5 million; its shares sold at Rs 2100, HNB (Non-Voting) 100,000 shares crossed to the tune of Rs 31.8 million; its shares traded at Rs 157.75, JKH one million shares crossed for Rs 22.3 million; its shares traded at Rs 22.30, ACL Cables 976000 shares crossed for Rs 21.4 million; its shares sold at Rs 219 and Access Engineering 300,000 shares crossed for Rs 21 million; its shares traded at Rs 70.

In the retail market top seven companies that mainly contributed to the turnover were; LOLC Rs 131.5 million (239,000 shares traded), Access Engineering Rs 103 million (1.5 million shares traded), ACL Cables Rs 87 million (395,000 shares traded), JKH Rs 71.7 million (3.2 million shares traded), Renuka Agri Foods Rs 66.6 million (7.4 million shares traded) Colombo Dockyards Rs 66.3 million (152,000 shares traded) and Commercial Credit and Finance Rs 60 million (460,000 shares traded). During the day 71.6 million share volumes changed hands in 22374 transactions.

It is said that the market performance was not satisfactory because of lower turnover and less investor participation. Top contributors came from the manufacturing sector counters, especially Hemas and JKH. The construction, banking and agriculture sectors performed well.

Yesterday the rupee was quoted at Rs 307.90/308.20 to the US dollar in the spot market, from 308.00/05 the previous day, dealers said, while bond yields were steady.
