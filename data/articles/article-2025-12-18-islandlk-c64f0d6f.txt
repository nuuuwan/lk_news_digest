Lack of post- Ditwah policy direction snags stock market vibrancy

CSE market activities were sluggish yesterday mainly due to the absence of a post-crisis economic policy direction. Besides, the authorities are yet to assess the total damage to the country stemming from the floods, market analysts said.

Amid those developments both indices indicated mixed reactions. The All Share Price Index went down by 3.56 points, while the S and P SL20 rose by 6.3 points. Turnover stood at Rs 3.3 billion with six crossings.

Those crossings were reported in Maharaja Foods , where Rs 10 million shares crossed to the tune of 130 million and its shares traded at Rs13, Capital Alliance Holdings 5 million shares crossed to the tune of Rs 85 million; its shares traded at Rs 17, Prime Lands Residencies 1.6 million shares crossed for Rs 50 million; its shares sold at Rs 31.30, Renuka Foods one million shares crossed to the tune of Rs 46.5 million; its shares traded at Rs 47.50, JKH 1 million shares crossed for Rs 21.2 million; its shares fetched Rs 21.20 and Commercial Bank 100,000 shares crossed for Rs 20 million; its shares traded at Rs 200.

In the retail market companies that mainly contributed to the turnover were; Renuka Agri Rs 754 million (71 million shares traded), Tokyo Cement Rs 242 million (2.2 million shares traded), LOLC Rs 125 million (220,000 shares traded), Tokyo Cement (Non-Voting) Rs 118 million (1.3 million shares traded), JKH Rs 110 million (5.2 million shares traded), Colombo Dockyard Rs 109 million (542,000 shares traded) and Capital Alliance Rs 92 million (1.4 million shares traded). During the day 157 million share volumes changed hands in 22831 transactions.

It is said that FMCG sector, especially Renuka Agri Foods and Maharaja Foods, were active in the market. Further, the real estate sector also performed well. The manufacturing sector, especially JKH, performed well too.

Meanwhile, LOLC Finance is listing Rs15 billion of 11.5 and 10.95 percent debentures, it said, in a stock exchange filing.

LOLC Finance raised Rs 15 billion through its issue of listed, rated, senior, unsecured, redeemable 5-year debentures.

Yesterday the rupee was trading at Rs 309.55/65 to the US dollar in the spot market, weaker from Rs 309.50/60 the previous day, dealers said, while bond yields opened lower.
