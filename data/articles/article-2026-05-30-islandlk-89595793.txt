La Trobe Forum examines AI’s impact on future jobs

La Trobe University, Colombo, in collaboration with Study Melbourne, Victoria, Australia, and La Trobe University, Australia, recently held a panel discussion on “AI and Future Employment Opportunities,” bringing together academics, education leaders and industry experts to examine how artificial intelligence is reshaping the global workforce.

The event, held at La Trobe University Colombo, focused on the evolving role of AI in employment, with discussions centred on emerging career pathways, digital skill requirements, automation trends, ethical considerations, and the transformation of business and healthcare sectors.

Participants included school principals, teachers, and higher education professionals, who shared perspectives on how students and educators can prepare for a rapidly changing job market driven by technological advancement.

Distinguished guests at the event included Michelle Wade, Victoria’s Commissioner to South Asia, Global Victoria, State Government of Victoria, Australia; David Walker, Associate Dean, Learning & Teaching, La Trobe Business School; Louise Lexis, Associate Dean, School of Agriculture, Biomedicine and Environment; Lisa Ritchie, Lecturer, School of Psychology and Public Health; Amit Malhotra, Senior Regional Director (South Asia and Africa); and Piyush Wahi, Associate Director (South Asia and Africa), La Trobe University.

Speaking at the forum, Michelle Wade highlighted the importance of collaboration between education providers and industry stakeholders in equipping students with the skills needed to navigate opportunities emerging from artificial intelligence and the changing world of work.

Professor Amalia Di Iorio, Pro Vice-Chancellor (Educational Partnerships), La Trobe University, said the partnership reflects a shared commitment to expanding access to high-quality international education through a globally aligned and locally responsive model. She noted that students are provided a pathway to begin their degrees in Sri Lanka while gaining skills relevant to an AI-driven global economy.

The organisers said the event highlighted La Trobe University’s commitment to raising awareness of emerging technological trends and preparing students, professionals and educators for future workforce demands.
