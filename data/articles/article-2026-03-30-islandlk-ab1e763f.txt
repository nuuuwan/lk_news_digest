Bourse down to just two crossings as bearishness takes hold

An escalation in Middle Eastern tensions yesterday underscored the CSE’s lingering bearishness and bred a dull sentiment among investors, market analysts said.

Brent futures were trading around US $ 115 a barrel, as Yemen’s Houthi rebels entered the West Asian conflict and additional US troops were deployed near Iran.

Amid those developments both indices moved upwards. The All Share Price Index went down by 254.78 points, while the S and P SL20 declined by 82.74 point.

Turnover stood at Rs 2.19 billion with two crossings. Those crossings were reported in Sampath Bank, where 615,000 shares crossed to the tune of Rs 95.7 million; its shares traded at Rs 155.50 and JKH 2.6 million shares crossed to the tune of Rs 2.6 million its shares traded at Rs 17.90.

In the retail market companies that mainly contributed to the turnover were; JKH Rs 299 million (16.6 million shares traded), Sampath Bank Rs 217 million (1.4 million shares traded), Lanka IOC Rs 182 million (103 million shares traded), Brown’s Investments Rs 120 million (19.7 million shares traded), LMF Rs 107 million (1.3 million shares traded), Colombo Dockyard Rs 98.9 million (806,000 shares traded) and Prime Lands Residencies Rs 94.6 million (1.9 million shares traded). During the day 100 million shares volumes changed hands in 25260 transactions.

It is said that manufacturing sector counters, especially JKH, led the market while the banking sector, especially Sampath Bank, was active at the floor. Further, the consumer goods and real estate sectors also performed well.

Yesterday the rupee was quoted at Rs 314.75/315.25 to the US dollar in the spot market, depreciating further from Rs 314.70/315.00 the previous day, dealers said, while bond yields opened slightly higher with the market a bit quiet.

A bond maturing on 15.12.2029 was quoted at 9.95/10.00 percent, up from 9.80/90 percent.

A bond maturing on 01.03.2030 was quoted at 10.00/03 percent, up from 9.90/10.00 percent.

A bond maturing on 15.03.2031 was quoted at 10.05/15 percent, up from 10.00/10 percent.

The rupee was quoted at 309.20/25 to the US dollar in the spot market on February 28, the day before the US and Israel bombed Iran.

The telegraphic transfer rates for the American dollar were 311.2500 buying, 318.2500 selling; the British pound was 411.4501 buying, and 422.7535 selling, and the euro was 355.8446 buying, 367.2640 selling.
