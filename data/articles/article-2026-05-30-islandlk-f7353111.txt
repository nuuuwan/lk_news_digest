NSB Group posts Rs. 15.5 Bn operating profit in Q1 2026

National Savings Bank (NSB) Group has reported an operating profit of Rs. 15.5 billion for the first quarter of 2026, underpinned by strong core banking income, robust capital buffers and stable asset quality indicators.

For the three months ended March 31, 2026, the Group recorded a profit before tax of Rs. 11.8 billion and profit after tax of Rs. 7 billion, while contributing Rs. 8.54 billion in taxes to the State during the period.

Total operating income rose to Rs. 22.5 billion, supported by an 8.1% year-on-year increase in net interest income to Rs. 21.8 billion and a 53.5% surge in net fee and commission income to Rs. 760.6 million.

Customer deposits stood at approximately Rs. 1.6 trillion, reinforcing the Bank’s position as a key custodian of national savings and a major contributor to financial intermediation. Loans and advances increased marginally to Rs. 556.1 billion, reflecting measured credit expansion.

The Bank’s capital and liquidity position remained strong, with the total capital adequacy ratio at 24.9%, Tier 1 capital ratio at 23.34%, liquidity coverage ratio at 335.54% and net stable funding ratio at 204.05%, all well above regulatory thresholds.

Asset quality showed improvement, with Stage 3 impaired loans declining to 2.3% and provision coverage rising to 59.2%, indicating strengthened credit risk management.

Chairman Dr. Harsha Cabral, PC, said the performance reflected public confidence in NSB and its role as a state-owned savings institution, noting its contribution of Rs. 8.4 billion in taxes during the quarter.

Acting General Manager/CEO Rohana Bandara Weerakoon said the results were driven by disciplined execution, prudent balance sheet management and sustained improvements in core income streams.
