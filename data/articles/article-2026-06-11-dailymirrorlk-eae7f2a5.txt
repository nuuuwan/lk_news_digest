Sri Lankan rupee edges lower against USD

Colombo, June 11 (Daily Mirror) - The Sri Lankan Rupee depreciated slightly against the US Dollar today (11) compared to Wednesday, with both buying and selling rates showing a marginal increase, according to the Central Bank of Sri Lanka.

The US Dollar selling rate rose to Rs. 337.82, up from Rs. 335.39, while the buying rate increased from Rs. 324.66 to Rs. 327.67.

The rupee also recorded a slight depreciation against a basket of other major foreign currencies, including the Australian and Canadian Dollars, the Euro, the British Pound Sterling and the Swiss Franc.
