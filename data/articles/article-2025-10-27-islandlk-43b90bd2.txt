Month-end profit-takings slow down share market, 13 crossings notwithstanding

The CSE activities were somewhat dull yesterday mainly due to month- end profit- takings, market analysts said.

The All Share Price Index went down by 9.43 points while the S and P SL20 declined by 6.33 points. Turnover stood at Rs 5.7 billion with 13 crossings.

Top seven crossings that mainly contributed to the turnover were; JKH nine million shares crossed to the tune of Rs 192 million; its shares traded at Rs 21.40, DFCC one million shares crossed for Rs 154 million; its shares traded at Rs 154, HNB 200,000 shares crossed for Rs 79 million; its shares sold at Rs 395, Seylan Development Bank 2.2 million shares crossed for Rs 66 million; its shares traded at Rs 30, Laugfs Gas one million shares crossed to the tune of Rs 62 million; its shares sold at Rs 62, Ambeon Capital 1.1 million shares crossed for Rs 50 million; its shares sold at Rs 45 and Cargills Ceylon 581,000 shares crossed for Rs 48.6 million; its shares fetched Rs 850.

In the retail market companies that mainly contributed to the turnover were; Colombo Dockyard Rs 442 million (1.7 million shares traded), Sierra Cables Rs 260 million (8.3 million shares traded), Ambeon Capital Rs 216 million (4.7 million shares traded), Lee Hedges Rs 147 million (138,000 shares traded) and JKH Rs 129 million (six million shares traded) and DFCC Bank Rs 128 million (785,000 shares traded). During the day 306 million share volumes changed hands in 37000 transactions.

It is said that the banking, financial and manufacturing sectors performed well.

Yesterday the rupee opened at Rs 303.90/304.00 to the US dollar, weaker from 303.70/85 last Friday, while bond yields were broadly steady, dealers said.

The telegraphic transfer rates for the American dollar were; 300.2500 buying, 307.2500 selling; the British pound was 399.0011 buying, and 410.3629 selling, and the euro was 347.0096 buying, 358.3728 selling.
