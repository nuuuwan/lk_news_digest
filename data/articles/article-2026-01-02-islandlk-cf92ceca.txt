Banking and construction sectors trigger bullish spurt in CSE

CSE activities were extremely bullish yesterday due to satisfactory investor participation which in turn resulted in market turnover improving, thus enabling active investor participation in the banking and construction sectors, market analysts said.

Amid those developments both indices moved upwards. The All Share Price Index went up by 286.15 points, while the S and P SL20 rose by 38.57 points. Turnover stood at Rs 5.17 billion with 10 crossings.

Top crossings were reported in Aitken Spence, where 800,000 shares crossed to the tune of Rs 122.2 million; its shares traded at Rs 154, ACL Cables 955,000 shares crossed to the tune of Rs 90.8 million; its shares traded at Rs 90.50, Dipped Products 831,000 shares crossed for Rs 53.6 million; its shares traded at

Rs 64.50, Laugfs Gas 604,000 shares crossed for Rs 44.1 million; its shares traded at Rs 73, Commercial Bank 200,000 shares crossed for Rs 40 million; its shares traded at Rs 200 and Sampath Bank 200,000 shares crossed for Rs 29.5 million; its shares sold at Rs 147.50.

In the retail market companies that mainly contributed to the turnover were; ACL Cables Rs 620 million (6.4 million shares traded), Sierra Cables Rs 395 million (10.7 million shares traded), Kalani Cables Rs 258 million (1.9 million shares traded), Royal Ceramics Rs 221 million (4.7 million shares traded),

Laugfs Gas Rs 157 million (2.3 million shares traded), Cable Solutions Rs 151 million (161,000 shares traded) and Prime Lands Residencies Rs 136 million (3.9 million shares traded). During the day 132.9 million share volumes changed hands in 39650 transactions.

It is said the manufacturing sector, especially ACL Cables, was active in the market, while the banking sector, especially Commercial Bank, proved active on the floor as well.

Yesterday the rupee traded at 309.75/85 to the US dollar in the spot market, weaker from 309.45/60 the previous day, dealers said, while bond yields extended recovery for the second consecutive day.

The rupee opened at Rs 309.30/60 before trending down, dealers said. Meanwhile,
