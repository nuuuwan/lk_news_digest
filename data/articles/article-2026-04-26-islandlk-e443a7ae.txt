OSC students take on global Slingshot Challenge to expose ocean acidification

Three students from The Overseas School of Colombo (OSC) have participated in the global Slingshot Challenge, supported by the National Geographic Society, which inspires youth to solve environmental issues through one-minute video presentations.

The students, part of the Wonder Lab after-school activity, focused on ocean conservation with their project titled “Sri Lanka’s Acidification Exposed.” Using cutting-edge technology, they investigated ocean acidification—an understudied concern in Sri Lankan waters.

“Through research and inquiry, we explored how rising carbon dioxide levels impact marine ecosystems,” said student Alessandro Nereo Francovigh. The team created a collection of visual stories inspired by National Geographic’s Story of Place approach to raise public awareness.

Fellow student Vasilissa Munasighe added: “Our motivation was a passion for science to create positive change within our community.”
