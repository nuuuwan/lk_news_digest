JAT Holdings’ subsidiary acquires New Zealand enterprise for Aust. dollars 4 million

JAT Holdings subsidiary, JAT Exports DMCC, acquired Mirotone (NZ) Limited of New Zealand for 4 million Australian dollars.

“This acquisition covers the company’s intellectual property portfolio, including proprietary formulations, brands, technology assets and international businesses of the company, CSE sources said.

Amid those developments both indices moved upwards yesterday. The All- Share Price Index went up by 114.15 points, while the S and P SL20 rose by 39.57 points. Turnover stood at Rs 5.7 billion with 11 crossings.

Top seven companies that mainly contributed to the turnover were; Digital Mobility Solutions 4.6 million shares crossed to the tune of Rs 691 million; its shares traded at Rs 154, Aitken Spence 3.3 million shares crossed for Rs 530 million; its shares traded at Rs 163, Jetwing Symphony 13.8 million shares crossed for Rs 257 million; its shares traded at Rs 19.90, Chevron Lubricants 379,000 shares crossed to the tune of Rs 70 million; its shares traded at Rs 185, Commercial Bank 300,000 shares crossed for Rs 61 million; its shares traded at Rs 207, Singha Hospital 4.4 million shares crossed for Rs 48.61 million; its shares sold at Rs 11 and Melstcope 250,000 shares crossed for Rs 47 million; its shares sold at Rs 190.

In the retail market top companies that contributed to the turnover were; Sierra Cables Rs 473.6 million (13.97 million shares traded), Chevron Lubricants Rs 247 million (1.3 million shares traded), Digital Mobility Solutions Rs 189.7 million (1.2 million shares traded), Colombo Dockyard Rs 158 million (455,000 shares traded), Lanka Milk Food Rs 138.5 million (1.9 million shares traded). During the day 158 million shares volumes changed hands in 30000 transactions.

It is said manufacturing sector, Sierra Cables and Chevron Lubricants played a significant role at the floor. Hotel sector counters also performed well, especially Jetwing Symphony.

Yesterday, the rupee opened at 304.45/55 to the US dollar in the spot market from Rs 304.35/45 the previous day, dealers said, while bond yields were steady.
