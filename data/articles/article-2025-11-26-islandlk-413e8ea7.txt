Conquering ups and downs share market ends on stable footing

The CSE kicked off on a positive note but later heavy selling pressure was noted due to month-end profit- takings but the market ended on a stable note yesterday, market analysts said.

Amid those developments both indices moved upwards. The All Share Price Index went up by 24.32 points while S and P SL20 rose by 19.53 points.

Turnover stood at Rs 3.44 billion with 12 crossings. Top seven crossings were reported in Hemas Holdings, where 4.2 million shares crossed to the tune of Rs 151 million and its shares traded at Rs 35.70, Colombo Dockyards 350,000 shares crossed to the tune of Rs 140 million; its shares traded at Rs 400, DFCC 596,000 shares crossed to the tune of Rs 95.7 million; its shares sold at Rs 160.50, Melstacorp 300,000 shares crossed to the tune of Rs 54.6 million; its shares traded at Rs 182, Commercial Bank 225,000 shares crossed for Rs 47 million and its shares traded at Rs 109 ,NTB 144,000 shares crossed for Rs 43.7 million; its shares sold at Rs 303 and Sampath Bank 250,000 shares crossed to the tune of Rs 36.9 million; its shares traded at Rs 47.75.

In the retail market top seven companies that mainly contributed to the turnover were; Access Engineering Rs 239 million (3.4 million shares traded), Hemas Rs 151 million (4.2 million shares traded), Colombo Dockyards Rs 140 million (350,000 shares traded), Hemas Holdings Rs 114 million (3.2 millions shares traded), DFCC Rs 95.7 million (596,000 shares traded), Citrus Waskaduwa Rs 97.4 million (42.5 million shares traded) and Colombo Dockyard Rs 85.2 million (207,000 shares traded). During the day 157 million share volumes changed hands in 27110 transactions.

Yesterday the rupee was quoted flat at Rs 307.90/308.00 to the US dollar in the spot market, dealers said, while short end bond yields edged up slightly and the long end of the yield curve held steady.
