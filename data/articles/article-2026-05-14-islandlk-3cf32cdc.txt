Vidullanka buys 49 percent stake in Storex for Rs 2 billion

Vidullanka said it entered into a share sale and purchase agreement and a shareholders’ agreement to buy a 49 percent equity stake in Storex (pvt) Limited for around Rs 2 billion with WindForce holding the remaining 51 percent stake.

The investment pertains to a portfolio of 12 BESS projects (each with a capacity of 10 Mw 40Mwh) awarded by the Ceylon Electricity Board to WindForce, CSE sources said.

The 12 locations awarded directly to WindForce PLC are being developed under Storex, WindForce said.

During the day CSE reported mixed reactions. The All Share Price Index went down by 3.0 points while S and P SL20 rose by 4.54 points. Turnover stood at Rs 2.59 billion with five crossings.

Those crossings were; Renuka Hotels 5.2 million shares crossed for Rs 280.6 million and its shares traded at Rs 54, HNB 202000 shares crossed for Rs 83.2 million; its shares traded at Rs 412, JKH two million shares crossed to the tune of Rs 41 million; its shares traded at Rs 20.50, Janashakthi 2.5 million shares crossed for Rs 35.3 million; its shares sold at Rs 14.10 and Commercial Bank 136,000 shares crossed to the tune of Rs 28.2 million; its shares traded at Rs 107.50.

In the retail market top seven companies that mainly contributed to the turnover were; ACL Cables Rs 218 .5 million (2.1 million shares traded), Alumax Rs 139 million (7.4 million shares traded) , JKH Rs 101 million (4.9 million shares traded), Cables Solutions Rs 88 million (6.4 million shares traded), Dialog Axiata Rs 81 million (2.3 million shares traded), CIC Holdings Rs 76 million (2.2 million shares traded) and Kernelhous Global Solutions Rs 49 million (one million shares traded). During the day 180.7 million share volumes changed hands in 20097 transactions.

Manufacturing sector counters, especially ACL Cables and JKH, performed well. Banking sector counters, HNB and Commercial Bank, performed well. Telecommunications sector, especially Dialog Axiata, also performed significantly.

Yesterday the rupee was quoted at Rs 324.50/325.00 to the US dollar in the spot market, after closing at Rs 326.00/327.00 to the US dollar on Wednesday, dealers said, while bond yields were steady.
