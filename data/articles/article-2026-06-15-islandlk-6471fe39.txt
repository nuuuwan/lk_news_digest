CanCham to launch dialogue on women’s economic empowerment and SME growth

The Canadian Chamber of Commerce in Sri Lanka (CanCham Sri Lanka) will convene a high-level dialogue on strengthening women’s development, economic empowerment and women-led Small and Medium Enterprises (SMEs) on June 17 at CanCham House in Colombo.

The initiative reflects the Chamber’s growing commitment to promoting inclusive economic growth by enhancing opportunities for women entrepreneurs and business leaders across Sri Lanka and the wider Indo-Pacific region.

The dialogue will feature Dr. Hina Shah, Founder of the International Centre for Entrepreneurship and Career Development (ICECD), an internationally acclaimed institution that has empowered thousands of women through entrepreneurship development, livelihood creation, skills enhancement and economic empowerment programmes.

CanCham Sri Lanka said women’s development remains a strategic priority for the Chamber, noting that stronger participation of women in the economy is essential for innovation, employment generation, poverty reduction and sustainable national development.

The event will also mark the launch of a new Chamber-led initiative aimed at fostering closer collaboration among stakeholders committed to advancing women’s entrepreneurship and supporting women-led enterprises.

According to organisers, the dialogue will review achievements, challenges and progress made in women’s economic empowerment in Sri Lanka while identifying practical measures to strengthen women’s participation in business and economic development.

Key discussions will focus on promoting the growth and competitiveness of women-led SMEs, facilitating networking and knowledge sharing among entrepreneurs from Sri Lanka, India, Canada and other Indo-Pacific countries, and building strategic partnerships involving the private sector, government agencies, development organisations, financial institutions and international stakeholders.

Participants will also explore opportunities available through the Canadian Chamber’s extensive network spanning Canada and the Indo-Pacific region.

A significant outcome expected from the dialogue is the commencement of a consultative process to formulate a comprehensive Five-Year Strategy and Action Plan for Women’s Development, Economic Empowerment and Women-Led SMEs in Sri Lanka.

CanCham Sri Lanka noted that the proposed strategy would seek to create a stronger ecosystem for women entrepreneurs, improve access to markets and finance, and contribute to long-term economic resilience and inclusive prosperity.
