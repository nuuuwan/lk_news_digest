CSE’s exuberance continues; turnover exceeds Rs 8 billion, indices up

The CSE yesterday too was in bullish mode and analysts attributed this to the optimism generated by the generally favourable economic prospects outlined for Sri Lanka by the IMF. Amid those developments both indices moved upwards. The All Share Price Index went up by143.98 points while S and P SL20 rose by 71.95 points. Turnover stood at Rs 8.5 billion with15 crossings.

Top seven crossings were; Mercantile Investments and Finance 180,000 shares crossed to the tune of Rs 990 million; its shares traded at Rs 5500, LOLC Holdings 517,000 shares crossed for Rs 291 million; its shares traded at Rs 5063, Capital Alliance 8.5 million shares crossed for Rs 157 million; its shares sold at Rs 80.30, DFCC 951,000 shares crossed to the tune of Rs 151 million; its shares sold at Rs 159.50, Laugfs Gas (Non- Voting), three million shares crossed to the tune of Rs 99 million; its shares sold at Rs 33, Sanasa Developments Bank 1.1 million shares crossed for Rs 55.8 million; its shares traded at Rs 51 and HNB (Non-Voting) 100,000 shares crossed for Rs 31 million; its shares fetched Rs 310.

In the retail market companies that mainly contributed to the turnover were; First Capital Holdings Rs 358 million (5.2 million shares traded), Digital Mobility Solutions Rs 339 million (2.2 million shares traded), Commercial Bank Rs 295 million (1.4 million shares traded), JKH Rs 267 million (12.5 million shares traded), ACL Cables Rs 229 million (1.05 million shares traded), SMB Leasing Rs 209 million (182 million shares traded) and Cargills Bank Rs 137 million (13.3 million shares traded).During the day 538 million share volumes changed hands in 44000 transactions.

It is said that the financial sector performed well, especially Mercantile Investments and Finance and First Capital Holdings. Banking sector too performed well.

Meanwhile, People’s Leasing & Finance said it has listed 55,491,496 new ordinary voting shares after a scrip dividend.

“Please note that 55,491,496 ordinary voting shares of the Company have been listed with effect from 10th October 2025, pursuant to a Scrip Dividend CSE sources said.

Yesterday, the rupee opened at Rs 302.70/80 to the US dollar, slightly stronger from Rs 302.75/83 the previous day, while bond yields were broadly steady, dealers said.
