Institutional investors at the ‘driving seat’ in stock trading

CSE trading indicated mixed reactions yesterday but the market was mainly driven by institutional investors, market analysts said.

Amid those developments both indices indicated mixed reactions. The All Share Price Index went up by 14.33 points, while the S and P SL20 went down by 4 points. Turnover stood at Rs 5.25 billion with five crossings.

Those crossings were; Softlogic Life 14.65 million shares crossed to the tune of Rs 1. 32 million and its shares traded at Rs 96, CTC 500,000 shares crossed for Rs 890 million; its shares traded at Rs 1780, Hayleys Fabrics five million shares crossed for Rs 154 million; its shares traded at Rs 30.80, CCS 800,000 shares crossed for Rs 92 million; its shares sold at Rs 115 and ACL Cables 294,000 shares crossed for Rs 28.4 million; its shares traded at Rs 96.50.

In the retail market top seven companies that mainly contributed to the turnover were; Softlogic Life Rs 156 million (1.7 million shares traded), Hayleys Fabrics Rs 152 million (5.3 million shares traded), Softlogic Capital Rs 144.8 million (8.4 million shares traded), HVA Foods Rs 121.8 million (14 million shares traded) Janashakthi Insurance Rs 113 million (2.1 million shares traded), Nawaloka Hospitals Rs 91.5 million (6.4 million shares traded) and NDB Rs 76.5 million (671,500 shares traded). During the day 155.5 million share volumes changed hands in 29949 transactions.

It is said that institutional investor participation showed a positive trend. Insurance sector counters, especially Softlogic Life performed well. Further manufacturing sector counters, especially Hayleys Fabrics performed well. Consumer and hospital sector counters also impressed.

Hayleys announced that its rights issue of ordinary voting shares has been oversubscribed. The company planned to raise Rs.9 billion through the issue of 45 million ordinary voting shares at Rs.200 per share. Total subscriptions, including applications for additional shares, reached 55,243,312 shares valued at Rs.11.05 billion.

Yesterday the rupee was quoted at Rs 316.60/317.00 to the US dollar in the spot market,from Rs 316.50/75 the previous day, dealers said, while bond yields edged lower.
