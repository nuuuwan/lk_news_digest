Parliament to take up Personal Data Protection (Amendment) Bill next week

Parliament will debate the Personal Data Protection (Amendment) Bill next week, following a decision by the Committee on Parliamentary Business.

Sessions will be held from 21 to 24 October, with the debate on the amendment scheduled for 21 October, along with the discussion on regulations under the National Medicines Regulatory Authority Act.

During the week, Parliament will also consider the Order under the Special Commodity Levy Act, rules under the Excise Ordinance, and an adjournment motion on the national program to eradicate drugs and organised crime and strengthen the legal framework.
