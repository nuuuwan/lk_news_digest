Cornell University students from US visit Brandix to explore the future of apparel

A group of students from Cornell University, New York, USA, recently stepped beyond the classroom and into the world of apparel manufacturing through a visit to Brandix and its facilities across Sri Lanka in January. The visit brought together PhD candidates and undergraduate students from Fashion Design, Fashion Design Management and Fiber Science within the Department of Human Centered Design (HCD) at Cornell University.

Designed as a hands-on academic–industry collaboration, the visit aimed to give students real-world exposure to how apparel is designed, developed, and manufactured at scale. More importantly, it offered insight into how innovation, sustainability, and ethical responsibility are shaping the future of the fashion industry.

Brandix was a natural partner for this engagement. As a global apparel solutions provider, the organisation operates at the intersection of agility, innovation, and sustainability. By opening its doors to students, Brandix continues to inspire the future change makers – providing them with the opportunity to test ideas, challenge assumptions, and understand the realities behind the products consumers wear every day.
