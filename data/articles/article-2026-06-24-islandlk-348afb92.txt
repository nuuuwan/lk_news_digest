Dull stock trading day marked by dip in indices

CSE trading commenced yesterday on a positive note but later declined into negativity, market analysts said.

The All Share Price Index went down by 47.91 points, while the S and P SL20 declined by 9.96 points. Turnover stood at Rs 1.19 billion with two crossings.

Those crossings were reported in Aitken Spence, where 150,000 shares crossed to the tune of Rs 23.7 million; its shares traded at Rs 158 and Central Industries 104,000 shares crossed for Rs 23.4 million; its shares traded at Rs 225.

In the retail market companies that mainly contributed to the turnover were; Bogala Graphite Rs 121 million (805,000,shares traded), LB Finance Rs 51 million (343,000 shares traded), Haycarb Rs 39 million (449,000 shares traded), Lankem Developments Rs 31 million (1.8 million shares traded), Aitken Spence Rs 31 million (196,000 shares traded) and HNB (Non-Voting) Rs 29 million (870,000 shares traded). During the day 48 million share volumes changed hands in 16775 transactions.

It is said that mixed market reactions were noted despite the low turnover level.

SANASA Life Insurance Company announced its intention to raise up to Rs 500 million via a private placement of up to five million unrated, unlisted, unsecured, Tier II, redeemable, cumulative, subordinated debentures.

Meanwhile, Diesel & Motor Engineering declared a final dividend of 40 rupees per share for the financial year ended March 31, 2026. Shares were trading 1,490.00, down 0.08 percent.

Yesterday the rupee was quoted at Rs 336.50/80 to the US dollar in the spot market, from Rs 335.15/40 the previous day, while bond yields were broadly steady, dealers said.

The telegraphic transfer rate for Sri Lanka’s rupee against the US dollar was 330.50 buying, 339.50 selling; the euro was 373.3996 selling, 387.3166 buying; and the pound was 435.1915 buying, 449.2371 selling.
