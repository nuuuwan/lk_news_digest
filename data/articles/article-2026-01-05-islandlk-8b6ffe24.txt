Share trading happily upwards, thanks to construction and banking counters

CSE activities yesterday were notably bullish and were mainly driven by construction and banking sector counters, market analysts said.

Due to the positive market performance both retail and institutional investments improved significantly, thus resulting in increased turnover.

The All Share Price Index went up by 150.67 points, while the S and P SL20 rose by 51.26 points. Turnover stood at Rs 5.77 billion with 11 crossings.

Top seven crossings were reported in ACL Cables where 1.59 million shares crossed to the tune of Rs 161 million; its shares traded at Rs 102, Cargills Ceylon 200,000 shares crossed to the tune of Rs 160 million; its shares sold at Rs 800, Dipped Products 1.14 million shares crossed to the tune of Rs 73.2 million; its shares sold at Rs 54.50, HNB (Non-Voting) 200,000 shares crossed for Rs 64 million; its shares traded at Rs 320, Access Engineering 680,000 shares crossed for Rs 53 million; its shares sold at Rs 78, Aitken Spence 250,000 shares crossed for Rs 40.5 million; its shares traded at Rs 156 and Sampath Bank 200,000 shares crossed for Rs 29.7 million; its shares sold at Rs 148.50.

In the retail market, companies thatmainly contributed to the turnover were; ACL Cables Rs 798 million (7.9 million shares traded), Commercial Bank Rs 481 million (1.4 million shares traded), Prime Lands Residencies Rs 210.5 million (5.98 million shares traded), Laugfs Gas Rs 205 million (2.7 million shares traded), Renuka Agri Rs 178.5 million (14 million shares traded), Sierra Cables Rs 160 million (4.3 million shares traded) and Kelani Cables Rs 140.6 million (970,000 shares traded). During the day 130.6 million shares volumes changed hands in 41683 transactions.

It is said manufacturing sector counters, especially ACL Cables, were active at the floor, while the banking sector, especially Commercial Bank, was active in the market. Further, the construction sector also performed in a significant manner.

Yesterday, the rupee was quoted at Rs 309.85/92 to the US dollar in the spot market , weaker from Rs 309.75/85 Friday, dealers said, while bond yields were somewhat steady.

The telegraphic transfer rates for the American dollar were 306.2500 buying, 313.2500 selling; the British pound was 410.5263 buying, and 421.8881 selling, and the euro was 355.9254 buying, 367.2886 selling.
