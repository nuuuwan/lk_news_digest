Tighter dollar conversion rule for exporters weighs on Colombo stock trading

The Central Bank’s directive requiring exporters to convert their dollars within one month – down from three months – amid rising depreciation pressure on the currency, sparked concerns among stock market participants yesterday, analysts said.

Amid those  developments the ASPI was down 85.54 points at 21,461.63, while the more liquid S&P SL20 was down 0.15 percent, or 9.12 points, at 5,991.56.

Market turnover was Ra 1.67 billion with five crossings.  Those crossings were reported in JKH, crossed 13.6 million shares to the tune of Rs 259 million and its share price traded at Rs 19.70, Janashakthi 4.5 million shares crossed to the tune of Rs  54 million and its share price traded at Rs 12, CCS 383,000 shares crossed to the tune of Rs 52  million and its share price traded at Rs 135.50, Cheveron Lubricant 185,500 shares crossed to the tune of Rs 37 million and its share price traded at Rs 190.75 and LMF 300,000 shares crossed to the tune of Rs 25.5 million and its share price traded at Rs 85.

In the retail market top seven companies that have mainly contributed to the turnover were Cheveron Lubricant Rs 166 million (852,000 shares traded), Commercial Bank Rs 113 million (554,000 shares traded), Pan Asia Power Rs 66 million (four million shares traded), JKH Rs 56 million (2.8 million shares traded), Prime Lands Residencies Rs 53 million (1.6 million shares traded), Cables Solutions Rs Rs 45 million (four million shares traded)  and Dialog Axiata Rs 43 million (970,000 shares traded). During the day 9.2 million shares volumes changed hands in 16705 transactions.Capital goods led turnover with  Rs 469.3 million .

Positive contributors to the ASPI were Bukit Darah (up 6.02 percent at 845.50 rupees), JKH (up 0.51 percent at Rs 19.80 ), Aitken Spence (up 1.48 percent at Rs 137.00 ), and Associated Motor Finance Company (up 3.33 percent at Rs 46.50 ).

CT Holdings (down 7.81 percent at Rs 525.50 ), Melstacorp (down 0.98 percent at Rs  177.25 ), and Janashakthi Insurance (down 7.79 percent at Rs 36.70 ) were top negative contributors.

In Asian regional markets, stock indices traded mixed. Hong Kong’s Hang Seng Index slipped 0.64 percent, or 157.94 points, to 24,407.96, and South Korea’s KOSPI fell 4.52 percent, or 366.11 points, to 7,730.82. Meanwhile, India’s NIFTY 50 edged up a marginal 0.079 percent, or 18.25 points, to close at 23,260.35

Yesterday the Central Bank announced the US Dollar rate as against rupee. The rupee closed at Rs 337.00/75 to the US dollar in the spot market on previous weaker from Rs 337.00/30 the previous day, dealers said, while bond yields closed higher.

The telegraphic transfer rate for Sri Lanka’s rupee against the US dollar was 332.50 buying, 341.50 selling; the euro was 381.2184 selling, 395.1354 buying; and the pound was 442.9204 buying, 456.9660 selling.
