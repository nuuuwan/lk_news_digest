CSE turnover capped by investor caution over external risks

Trading on the Colombo Stock Exchange (CSE) yesterday edged higher in early dealings, but the uptick failed to build sustained momentum as mounting external risks kept investors cautious.

Market analysts said lingering concerns over U.S. President Donald Trump’s proposed 10% across-the-board tariff damped appetite for risk assets, with institutional investors remaining largely on the sidelines.

Amid those developments both indices moved downwards. All Share Price Index down by 92.4 points while S and P SL20 down by 39.22 points.

Those crossings were reported in Dialog Axiata 19 million shares crossed to the tune of Rs 626.7 million and its share price traded at Rs 33, ACL Cables 195,000 shares crossed to the tune of Rs 71.6 million and its share price traded at Rs 103, Access Engineering 908,000 shares crossed to the tune of Rs 69 million and its share price traded at Rs 76, Amana Tackful three million shares crossed to the tune of Rs 67 million and its share price traded at Rs 22.50, Hemas Holdings 815,000 shares crossed to the tune of Rs 27.4 million and its share price traded at Rs 33.60, JKH one million shares crossed to the tune of Rs 22.2 million and its share price traded at Rs 22.20 and Tokyo Cement (Non-Voting) 230,000 shares crossed to the tune of Rs 20.2 million and its share price traded at Rs 88.

In the retail market top seven companies that have mainly contributed to the turnover were ACL Cables Rs 268.7 million (2.6 million shares traded), Asia Siyaka Rs 246.9 million (14 million shares traded), Access Engineering Rs 158.3 million (two million shares traded), Acme Printers Rs 94.8 million (30.8 million shares traded), JKH Rs 92.9 million (4.2 million shares traded),Colombo Dockyards Rs 84.5 million (551,000 shares traded) and Sampath Bank Rs 84 million (518,000 shares traded). During the day 152.5 million shares volumes changed hands in 31244 transactions.

It is said that mixed reactions noted throughout the day. Telecommunications sector especially Dialog Axiata active in the market while manufacturing sector counters especially JKH performed well.

Yesterday the Central Bank announced the US Dollar rate as against rupee. The rupee was quoted at Rs 309.35/40 to the US dollar in the spot market, from Rs. 309.36/38 the previous day, dealers said, while bond yields edged up slightly.
