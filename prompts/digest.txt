You will be given several Sri Lankan news articles from the past few days. Write a
concise, cohesive editorial digest in the style of The Economist.

Go beyond summary — analyze and interpret the week's developments into a unified
narrative. The subheadings, read in sequence, should themselves tell a coherent story
arc about Sri Lanka's week.

Use markdown level 2 headings (`##`) as the subheadings. Each heading must be:
(a) a complete, standalone, declarative sentence anchored in verifiable fact;
(b) written in the register of a Financial Times or Economist headline — precise,
active, and economical; and (c) part of a logical narrative progression when read in
order. Follow each heading with a short, descriptive paragraph (not bullets) that adds
context and nuance.

Do not include a summary or conclusion subheading. Highlight key facts and trends in
**bold**. Anchor numbers and statistics in context in parentheses for meaning. Use
emojis purposefully for tone or clarity. Explain non-obvious acronyms, jargon, and
technical terms in parentheses on first use.

Stick strictly to verifiable facts from the provided articles. Exclude opinion,
speculation, and editorial judgment. Follow correct GitHub Markdown styling (e.g. blank
line after headings, blank line between sections).
